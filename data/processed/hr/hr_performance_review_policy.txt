PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT POLICY

1. Annual Reviews
All employees receive a formal performance review annually. Failure by managers
to complete required performance reviews constitutes a management conduct issue
subject to HR intervention.

2. Performance Improvement Plan
Employees whose performance falls below the required standard will be placed on
a formal Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) of 60 to 90 days. Failure to achieve
PIP targets may result in demotion or termination of employment.

3. Rating Process
Performance ratings must be based on objective evidence. Discrimination, bias,
or personal relationships must not influence ratings. Managers found to have
manipulated ratings are subject to disciplinary action.

4. Underperformance
Persistent underperformance after a PIP without improvement constitutes grounds
for dismissal for capability. The standard disciplinary process does not apply
to capability dismissals.

5. Retaliation
Retaliating against an employee for raising concerns about the performance
review process is prohibited and constitutes gross misconduct.
