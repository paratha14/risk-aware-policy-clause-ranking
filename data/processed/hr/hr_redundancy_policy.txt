REDUNDANCY POLICY

1. Selection Criteria
Redundancy selection must be based on objective, non-discriminatory criteria
such as skills, performance, and attendance. Selection based on protected
characteristics constitutes unlawful discrimination and exposes the organisation
to tribunal claims.

2. Consultation
A minimum consultation period of 30 days is required for 20 or more redundancies.
Failure to consult constitutes a breach of statutory obligations and may result
in a protective award of up to 90 days' pay per affected employee.

3. Redundancy Pay
Eligible employees are entitled to statutory redundancy pay calculated by age
and length of service. Withholding lawful redundancy pay is unlawful and may
result in employment tribunal proceedings.

4. Alternative Employment
The company must consider and offer suitable alternative employment where it
exists. Unreasonable refusal of a suitable alternative offer by an employee
may disqualify them from statutory redundancy pay.

5. Appeal
Employees have the right to appeal redundancy selection within 10 working days.
Failure to provide an appeal process constitutes a breach of the ACAS Code of
Practice and may increase tribunal awards.
