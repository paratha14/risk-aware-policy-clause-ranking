PROBATIONARY PERIOD POLICY

1. Duration
All new employees serve a probationary period of 6 months. The probationary
period may be extended by up to 3 months where performance or conduct concerns
have been identified.

2. Performance Standards
Employees must demonstrate satisfactory performance, attendance, and conduct
during probation. Failure to meet required standards during probation may result
in termination of employment without following the full disciplinary procedure.

3. Review Meetings
Formal probationary reviews are conducted at 3 and 6 months. Failure to attend
a scheduled probationary review without prior notice may be treated as a conduct
issue.

4. Notice During Probation
The notice period during probation is one week on either side. The company
reserves the right to terminate employment during probation without following
the standard disciplinary procedure where performance is unsatisfactory.

5. Confirmation of Employment
Confirmation of employment is not automatic. Written confirmation must be
received before the employee is considered to have passed probation.
