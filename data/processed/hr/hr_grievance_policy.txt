GRIEVANCE POLICY

1. Right to Raise a Grievance
All employees have the right to raise a grievance about their work, working
conditions, or treatment by colleagues or management without fear of retaliation.
Victimisation of employees who raise grievances constitutes gross misconduct.

2. Informal Resolution
Employees are encouraged to resolve grievances informally with their line manager
in the first instance. Where informal resolution is not appropriate or has failed,
a formal grievance may be submitted.

3. Formal Grievance Procedure
Formal grievances must be submitted in writing to HR. A grievance hearing will
be arranged within 10 working days. The employee has the right to be accompanied
by a trade union representative or colleague.

4. Outcome and Appeal
The outcome of a grievance investigation will be communicated in writing within
5 working days of the hearing. Employees may appeal the outcome within 10 working
days. The appeal decision is final.

5. Malicious Complaints
Employees who submit grievances found to be deliberately false or malicious may
be subject to disciplinary action.
