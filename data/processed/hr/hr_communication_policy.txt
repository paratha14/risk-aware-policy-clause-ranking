WORKPLACE COMMUNICATION POLICY

1. Professional Standards
All workplace communications including email, messaging platforms, and written
correspondence must be professional, respectful, and free from offensive content.
Communications that constitute harassment, discrimination, or bullying are gross
misconduct offences.

2. Email Use
Company email accounts must be used for business purposes only. Personal use
is permitted on a minimal basis but must not involve inappropriate, illegal, or
offensive content. The company reserves the right to monitor email accounts on
company systems.

3. Confidentiality in Communications
Employees must not include confidential company information in external emails
without encryption or explicit authorisation. Inadvertent disclosure of confidential
information through email must be reported to the IT department immediately.

4. Legal Holds
Where litigation is anticipated, employees must preserve all relevant communications
and must not delete emails or messages related to the matter. Destruction of
potentially relevant communications constitutes spoliation and may result in
serious legal consequences.

5. Record Keeping
Employees in regulated roles must ensure that material communications are recorded
in accordance with applicable regulatory requirements. Failure to maintain required
records is a regulatory offence and a disciplinary matter.
