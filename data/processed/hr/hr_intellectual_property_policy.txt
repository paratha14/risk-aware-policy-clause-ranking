INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY POLICY

1. Ownership
All intellectual property created by employees in the course of their employment
belongs to the company. This includes inventions, software, designs, written works,
and trade secrets created using company resources or during working hours.

2. Disclosure
Employees must disclose all potentially patentable inventions or commercially
valuable intellectual property to the company immediately upon creation. Failure
to disclose may result in disciplinary action and legal proceedings.

3. Personal Projects
Employees wishing to develop intellectual property in their own time must obtain
prior written clearance where the work is related to the company's field of
business. Failure to obtain clearance may result in the company asserting ownership.

4. Third-Party IP
Employees must not incorporate third-party intellectual property into company
work without appropriate licensing. Infringement of third-party IP rights exposes
the company to legal liability and may result in disciplinary action against the
responsible employee.

5. Post-Employment
Obligations regarding protection of company intellectual property and trade secrets
continue after termination of employment. Misuse of company IP after departure may
result in injunctive relief and claims for damages.
