DRUG AND ALCOHOL POLICY

1. Prohibition
Employees must not be under the influence of alcohol or illegal drugs while at
work, on company premises, or while operating company vehicles. Attendance at
work while impaired is gross misconduct subject to immediate dismissal.

2. Testing
The company reserves the right to conduct drug and alcohol testing where there
is reasonable cause or following a workplace accident. Refusal to submit to
testing without a valid medical reason is treated as a positive result and
may result in dismissal.

3. Prescription Medication
Employees taking prescription medication that may affect their performance or
ability to operate machinery must notify occupational health. Failure to disclose
relevant medication that subsequently causes an incident may be treated as
a disciplinary matter.

4. Social Events
Excessive alcohol consumption at company social events that results in misconduct
is subject to the same disciplinary procedures as misconduct at the workplace.

5. Support
The company encourages employees with substance misuse issues to seek support.
Self-referral to the employee assistance programme will be treated confidentially
and will not automatically trigger disciplinary action.
