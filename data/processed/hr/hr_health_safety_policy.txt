HEALTH AND SAFETY POLICY

1. Employer Obligations
The organisation is committed to providing a safe working environment. Failure
to maintain required safety standards may result in regulatory enforcement action,
fines, and legal liability.

2. Employee Responsibilities
Employees must comply with all health and safety procedures. Failure to follow
safety procedures, tampering with safety equipment, or creating hazardous
conditions constitutes gross misconduct subject to immediate dismissal.

3. Incident Reporting
All workplace accidents, near-misses, and hazards must be reported immediately.
Failure to report incidents is a disciplinary offence. Falsifying incident reports
constitutes gross misconduct.

4. Risk Assessments
Managers must ensure risk assessments are completed and reviewed annually.
Conducting high-risk activities without a valid risk assessment is prohibited
and may result in regulatory prosecution.

5. Alcohol and Drugs
Attending work under the influence of alcohol or non-prescribed drugs is a gross
misconduct offence subject to immediate dismissal. The organisation reserves the
right to require drug and alcohol testing where there is reasonable suspicion.
