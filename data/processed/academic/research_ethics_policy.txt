RESEARCH ETHICS AND INTEGRITY POLICY

1. Research Misconduct
Research misconduct includes fabrication, falsification, and plagiarism (FFP).
Any researcher found guilty of research misconduct shall face immediate suspension
from research activities, retraction of published work, and may be subject to
termination of employment or expulsion. Cases of serious misconduct are referred
to relevant professional bodies and funding agencies.

2. Data Management
All research data must be stored securely and retained for a minimum of 10 years
after publication. Destruction of research data to avoid scrutiny constitutes
research misconduct and is subject to disciplinary action.

3. Authorship
Authorship must reflect genuine intellectual contribution. Gift authorship and
ghost authorship are prohibited and constitute a form of academic misconduct
subject to formal investigation.

4. Conflict of Interest
Researchers must disclose all financial and personal conflicts of interest before
commencing research. Failure to disclose conflicts may result in retraction of
findings and disciplinary action.

5. Ethical Approval
All research involving human subjects must receive ethics committee approval
before commencement. Conducting research without approval is a serious violation
subject to immediate suspension and potential legal action.
