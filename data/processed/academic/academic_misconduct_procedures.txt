ACADEMIC MISCONDUCT INVESTIGATION PROCEDURES

1. Initiation of Investigation
An academic misconduct investigation is initiated upon receipt of a formal report
from a faculty member, invigilator, or another student. All reports must be
submitted in writing within 10 working days of the alleged incident. Late reports
require justification and may not be accepted.

2. Notification
The accused student will be notified in writing within 5 working days of the
investigation being opened. Failure to respond to notification within 10 working
days will result in the investigation proceeding in the student's absence.

3. Evidence Gathering
The investigating officer will gather all relevant evidence including submitted
work, examination scripts, digital logs, and witness statements. Destruction
or tampering with evidence constitutes a further act of misconduct and may result
in enhanced penalties.

4. Hearing
A formal hearing will be convened within 20 working days. The student has the
right to attend, present their case, and be accompanied by a student union
representative. Refusal to attend without valid reason is treated as an admission.

5. Penalties
Penalties range from a formal written warning to expulsion depending on severity:
- First minor offence: written warning and compulsory academic integrity training
- Serious or repeat offence: failure of module or course
- Gross misconduct (contract cheating, impersonation): expulsion from the university
