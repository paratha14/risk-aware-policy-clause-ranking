STUDENT CODE OF CONDUCT

Section 1 Prohibited Conduct
The following conduct is strictly prohibited and will result in formal
disciplinary action:
(a) Physical violence or threats of violence against any university community member
(b) Harassment, bullying, or discriminatory behaviour on any protected ground
(c) Unauthorized access to university IT systems or restricted facilities
(d) Theft or wilful damage to university property or property of others
(e) Possession, use, or distribution of illegal substances on campus
(f) Forgery, falsification, or misrepresentation in academic or administrative matters

Section 2 Sanctions
Students found in violation of this Code may face:
- Formal written warning placed on academic record
- Community service requirement
- Suspension of campus privileges
- Academic suspension for up to two semesters
- Permanent expulsion from the university

Section 3 Appeals Process
Students may appeal disciplinary decisions within 14 working days of notification.
Appeals must be submitted in writing with supporting documentation. The Appeals
Committee's decision is final and binding. No further internal appeal is available.

Section 4 Substance Misuse
Possession or distribution of illegal substances on campus is a critical violation.
First-time offenders face mandatory counselling referral and probationary period.
Repeat offenders are subject to immediate suspension pending investigation and
possible expulsion.
