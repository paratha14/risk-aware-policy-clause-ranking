FIELD TRIP AND OFF-CAMPUS ACTIVITY POLICY

1. Risk Assessment
All off-campus activities must have a completed and approved risk assessment before
departure. Conducting a field trip without an approved risk assessment is prohibited
and constitutes a serious health and safety violation.

2. Student Consent
Participation in field trips requires signed informed consent. Students who participate
without submitting a valid consent form may be required to leave the activity at their
own expense.

3. Code of Conduct
Students on field trips represent the university and must comply with the student
code of conduct at all times. Misconduct on field trips is subject to the same
disciplinary procedures as on-campus misconduct.

4. Alcohol and Substances
Consumption of alcohol by students under the legal drinking age during field trips
is strictly prohibited. Possession or use of illegal substances during any university
activity results in immediate removal and formal disciplinary proceedings.

5. Accidents and Incidents
All accidents, near-misses, and incidents during field trips must be reported to the
trip leader immediately and documented. Failure to report incidents may affect
insurance coverage and constitutes a breach of university health and safety policy.
