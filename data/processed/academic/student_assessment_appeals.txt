ASSESSMENT APPEALS AND COMPLAINTS POLICY

1. Grounds for Appeal
Students may appeal assessment decisions on the following grounds only:
(a) Procedural irregularity in the conduct of the assessment
(b) Circumstances affecting performance that were not disclosed before the assessment
(c) Administrative error in recording or calculating the result

Disagreement with the academic judgment of examiners is not grounds for appeal.

2. Appeal Deadlines
Appeals must be submitted within 20 working days of the release of results.
Late appeals will not be considered except in exceptional documented circumstances.

3. Frivolous Appeals
Students submitting appeals found to be frivolous, vexatious, or without
foundation may receive a formal warning. Repeat frivolous appeals may result
in restrictions on future appeal rights.

4. Academic Misconduct Appeals
Appeals against academic misconduct findings follow a separate process. The
standard of proof is balance of probabilities. The Appeals Panel decision is
final. No further internal appeal is available after the Panel has ruled.

5. Exam Irregularities
Disruption of examinations including talking, use of unauthorized devices, or
leaving without permission is subject to immediate removal and potential
disqualification from the assessment.
