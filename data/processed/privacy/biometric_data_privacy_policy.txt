BIOMETRIC DATA COLLECTION AND USE POLICY
Based on: Illinois Biometric Information Privacy Act (BIPA, 740 ILCS 14),
GDPR Article 9, and CCPA Biometric Data provisions

1. Informed Consent
Biometric identifiers — including fingerprints, retina scans, facial geometry, and
voiceprints — must not be collected without first providing a written policy and
obtaining written consent. Collection of biometric data without consent violates BIPA
and subjects the entity to statutory damages of $1,000 per negligent violation and
$5,000 per intentional or reckless violation, plus attorney fees.

2. Retention Schedule
Biometric data must be permanently destroyed when the initial purpose for collection
has been satisfied or within 3 years of the individual's last interaction, whichever
occurs first. Retention of biometric data beyond permitted periods violates BIPA and
constitutes a separate, actionable violation per data subject per retention period.

3. Prohibition on Sale or Profit
Biometric data must not be sold, leased, traded, or otherwise profited from under
any circumstances. Any commercial transaction involving biometric identifiers or
information is a per se violation of BIPA and subjects the entity to significant
statutory damages.

4. Data Security
Biometric data must be stored using a reasonable standard of care no less protective
than the standard used to protect other confidential and sensitive information.
Failure to adequately secure biometric data is an independent violation.

5. Third-Party Disclosure
Biometric data must not be disclosed or disseminated to third parties without the
data subject's consent or as required by law. Any unauthorised third-party disclosure
constitutes a violation and subjects the disclosing party to statutory damages.
