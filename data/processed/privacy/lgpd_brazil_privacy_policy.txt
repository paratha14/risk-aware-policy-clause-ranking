LGPD — BRAZIL GENERAL DATA PROTECTION LAW COMPLIANCE POLICY
Based on: Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados Pessoais (Law No. 13,709/2018)

1. Legal Bases for Processing
Personal data may only be processed on one of the ten legal bases established by
Article 7 of the LGPD. Processing without a valid legal basis is unlawful. The
Brazilian Data Protection Authority (ANPD) may apply administrative sanctions
including fines of up to 2% of revenue in Brazil, limited to R$50 million per
infraction.

2. Data Subject Rights
Data subjects have the right to confirm the existence of processing, access their
data, correct inaccurate data, anonymise or delete unnecessary data, obtain
portability, and object to processing. Controllers must respond to requests within
15 days. Failure to respond or unlawful denial of rights is subject to ANPD
investigation and sanctions.

3. Sensitive Personal Data
Processing of sensitive personal data — including health data, biometric data,
racial or ethnic origin, religious conviction, and political opinion — requires
explicit and specific consent or another legal basis permitted by Article 11 LGPD.
Unlawful processing of sensitive data attracts enhanced scrutiny and sanctions.

4. Data Protection Officer
Organisations processing personal data must appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO).
The DPO's identity and contact details must be publicly disclosed. Failure to appoint
or disclose a DPO is a violation subject to administrative warning.

5. Cross-Border Data Transfers
Personal data may only be transferred internationally to countries with adequate
protection or where appropriate guarantees are in place. Transfers without adequate
safeguards are prohibited and subject to sanctions including suspension of processing.
