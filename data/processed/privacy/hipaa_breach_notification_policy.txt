HIPAA BREACH NOTIFICATION POLICY
Based on: 45 CFR §§ 164.400–164.414 (HITECH Act Breach Notification Rule, 2009)

1. Definition of Breach
A breach is an impermissible use or disclosure of unsecured PHI that compromises
its security or privacy. All impermissible uses or disclosures are presumed to be
breaches unless the covered entity demonstrates a low probability that PHI has been
compromised based on a four-factor risk assessment.

2. Individual Notification
Affected individuals must be notified without unreasonable delay and no later than
60 calendar days following discovery of a breach. Notification must be written and
must describe the breach, the types of information involved, steps individuals should
take, and what the entity is doing to investigate and mitigate harm.

3. Media Notification
For breaches affecting more than 500 residents of a state or jurisdiction, covered
entities must notify prominent media outlets serving the affected area. Media
notification must occur without unreasonable delay and within 60 days of discovery.

4. HHS Notification
All breaches must be reported to the Secretary of HHS. Breaches affecting 500 or
more individuals must be reported within 60 days of discovery. Breaches affecting
fewer than 500 individuals may be reported annually. Failure to report constitutes
an independent violation of the HIPAA Rules.

5. Penalties for Non-Notification
Failure to provide required breach notifications subjects the covered entity to civil
monetary penalties tiered by culpability: from $100 per violation for unknowing
violations to $50,000 per violation for wilful neglect that is not corrected.
