DATA RETENTION AND SECURE DISPOSAL POLICY
Based on: GDPR Article 5(1)(e), UK GDPR, and ISO 27001 Data Management Standards

1. Retention Schedule Compliance
Personal data must be retained only for as long as necessary for the purposes for
which it was collected. Organisations must maintain a documented retention schedule
for all categories of personal data. Retention of personal data beyond scheduled
periods without lawful basis is a violation of the storage limitation principle and
subject to supervisory authority enforcement action.

2. Secure Deletion
When personal data reaches its scheduled retention end-date, it must be securely
deleted or anonymised. Hard deletion must render data unrecoverable. Disposal methods
must be documented and verifiable. Failure to securely delete personal data is a
violation subject to ICO investigation.

3. Backup Data
Personal data held in backup systems is subject to the same retention obligations as
live data. Organisations must implement processes to delete personal data from backups
within a reasonable period of the scheduled retention end-date. Indefinite retention
in backups is not compliant with GDPR storage limitation requirements.

4. Subject Access and Deletion Requests
On receipt of a valid erasure request under GDPR Article 17, personal data must be
deleted without undue delay unless a retention exemption applies. Failure to process
erasure requests is a violation of data subject rights subject to supervisory authority
investigation and enforcement.

5. Physical Media Disposal
Physical media containing personal data must be disposed of securely using methods
that prevent recovery, such as cross-cut shredding or degaussing. Placing media
containing personal data in general waste is a serious data protection violation
subject to significant fines and reputational damage.
