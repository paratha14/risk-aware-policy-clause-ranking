AI AND AUTOMATED DECISION-MAKING PRIVACY POLICY
Based on: GDPR Article 22, EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689), and ICO AI Guidance

1. Right Not to Be Subject to Automated Decisions
Individuals have the right not to be subject to decisions based solely on automated
processing — including profiling — that produce legal or similarly significant effects.
Processing in violation of this right is unlawful under GDPR Article 22 and subject to
supervisory authority enforcement and fines.

2. High-Risk AI Systems
Operators of high-risk AI systems as defined under the EU AI Act must comply with
conformity assessment requirements before deployment. Placing a non-compliant
high-risk AI system on the market is a serious violation subject to fines of up to
€30 million or 6% of global annual turnover.

3. Transparency Obligations
Where automated decision-making is used, individuals must be provided with meaningful
information about the logic involved, the significance, and the envisaged consequences.
Failure to provide adequate transparency constitutes a violation of GDPR Article 13/14.

4. Human Oversight
High-risk AI systems must be designed to allow effective oversight by natural persons.
Deployment of high-risk AI systems without adequate human oversight mechanisms violates
the EU AI Act and may result in prohibition orders and financial penalties.

5. Prohibited AI Practices
Certain AI practices are prohibited outright under the EU AI Act, including subliminal
manipulation, exploitation of vulnerabilities, and real-time remote biometric
identification in public spaces with limited exceptions. Deployment of prohibited AI
systems is a critical violation subject to the highest penalty tier.
