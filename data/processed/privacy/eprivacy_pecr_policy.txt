EPRIVACY AND PECR COMPLIANCE POLICY
Based on: Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations 2003 (PECR) and
ePrivacy Directive 2002/58/EC as amended by 2009/136/EC

1. Cookie Consent
Non-essential cookies and similar tracking technologies must not be placed on a
user's device without prior, freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous
consent. Placing tracking cookies without valid consent is a violation of PECR.
The ICO may issue fines of up to £500,000 and enforcement notices for PECR breaches.

2. Consent Mechanisms
Consent must be obtained through a clear affirmative action. Pre-ticked boxes, consent
obtained through continued browsing, or consent bundled with terms of service does not
constitute valid consent under PECR. Organisations using invalid consent mechanisms
must remediate immediately upon notification.

3. Direct Marketing
Electronic marketing communications must only be sent to individuals who have
specifically consented to receive them, or where the soft opt-in exemption applies.
Sending unsolicited direct marketing without valid consent is a violation subject to
ICO fines and enforcement action.

4. Right to Withdraw Consent
Individuals have the right to withdraw consent for cookies and direct marketing at any
time. Withdrawal must be as easy as giving consent. Failure to honour withdrawal
requests is a violation of PECR.

5. Subscriber and Traffic Data
Traffic and location data generated by electronic communications services must be
erased or anonymised when no longer needed for transmission. Retention of traffic
data beyond permitted periods is a violation of the ePrivacy Directive and PECR.
