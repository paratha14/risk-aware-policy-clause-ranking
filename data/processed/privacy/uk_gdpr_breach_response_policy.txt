UK GDPR DATA BREACH RESPONSE POLICY
Based on: UK GDPR Articles 33–34 and ICO Breach Notification Guidance

1. Internal Reporting
All staff who become aware of a potential personal data breach must report it to the
Data Protection Officer (DPO) immediately and no later than within 24 hours of
discovery. Failure to report internally may result in disciplinary action and could
prevent the organisation from meeting its regulatory notification obligations.

2. ICO Notification
Personal data breaches that are likely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms
of individuals must be reported to the ICO within 72 hours of becoming aware of the
breach. Failure to notify within 72 hours without good reasons is a violation subject
to ICO reprimand and fines of up to £8.7 million or 2% of global turnover.

3. Individual Notification
Where a breach is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of
individuals, those individuals must be notified without undue delay. Failure to notify
affected individuals when required constitutes a serious violation subject to
enforcement action.

4. Documentation
All breaches, whether notifiable or not, must be documented in the organisation's
breach register. Failure to maintain adequate breach records is a violation of the
UK GDPR accountability principle.

5. Post-Breach Review
Following any significant breach, the organisation must conduct a root cause analysis
and implement appropriate remedial measures. Failure to take remedial action following
a breach may be treated as an aggravating factor in any subsequent ICO investigation.
