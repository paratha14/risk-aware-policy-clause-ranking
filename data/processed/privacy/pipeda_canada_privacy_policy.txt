PIPEDA — CANADA PERSONAL INFORMATION PROTECTION COMPLIANCE POLICY
Based on: Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (S.C. 2000, c. 5)

1. Consent Requirement
Organisations must obtain meaningful consent before collecting, using, or disclosing
personal information. Consent obtained through deceptive or misleading practices is
not valid. Failure to obtain valid consent is a violation of PIPEDA and may result in
an OPC investigation and Federal Court orders requiring compliance.

2. Limiting Collection
Only information necessary for the identified purposes must be collected. Collection
of personal information beyond identified purposes is a violation of the limiting
collection principle and subject to OPC investigation and findings of non-compliance.

3. Breach of Security Safeguards
Since November 2018, organisations must report breaches of security safeguards that
pose a real risk of significant harm to the Privacy Commissioner of Canada and notify
affected individuals. Failure to report a qualifying breach is an offence subject to
fines of up to $100,000 CAD.

4. Access and Correction Rights
Individuals have the right to know what personal information an organisation holds
about them and to challenge its accuracy. Organisations must respond to access
requests within 30 days. Unjustified denial of access or correction rights is subject
to OPC complaint investigation and Federal Court proceedings.

5. Accountability
Organisations are responsible for personal information under their control, including
information disclosed to third parties for processing. Organisations must implement
contractual protections with third parties. Failure to maintain accountability for
third-party processing is a violation of PIPEDA's accountability principle.
