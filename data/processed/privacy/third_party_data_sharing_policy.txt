THIRD-PARTY DATA SHARING AND PROCESSOR POLICY
Based on: GDPR Articles 26–29, UK GDPR, and ICO Accountability Framework

1. Controller-Processor Contracts
Where personal data is processed by a third party on behalf of the organisation,
a written Data Processing Agreement (DPA) must be in place before processing commences.
Engaging a processor without a compliant DPA is a violation of GDPR Article 28 and
subject to supervisory authority enforcement action and fines.

2. Processor Due Diligence
Controllers must only appoint processors that provide sufficient guarantees of
appropriate technical and organisational measures. Engaging a processor without
adequate due diligence is a violation of GDPR accountability requirements.

3. Sub-Processing
Processors must not engage sub-processors without prior specific or general written
authorisation from the controller. Unauthorised sub-processing is a violation of the
DPA and GDPR Article 28(2), giving rise to liability for both processor and controller.

4. Joint Controller Arrangements
Where two or more controllers jointly determine the purposes and means of processing,
a transparent arrangement must be documented in writing. Failure to establish and
communicate a joint controller arrangement is a violation of GDPR Article 26.

5. Data Sharing Agreements
Personal data shared with third-party controllers for independent processing requires
a lawful basis and, where required, a data sharing agreement. Unlawful sharing of
personal data with third parties is a serious violation subject to the highest penalty
tier under GDPR.
