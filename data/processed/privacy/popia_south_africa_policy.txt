POPIA — SOUTH AFRICA PROTECTION OF PERSONAL INFORMATION COMPLIANCE POLICY
Based on: Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (POPIA)

1. Conditions for Lawful Processing
Personal information may only be processed if it is adequate, relevant, and not
excessive; collected for a specific purpose; and processed in a manner compatible
with that purpose. Unlawful processing may result in enforcement notices, fines of
up to R10 million, or imprisonment of up to 10 years for responsible parties.

2. Security Safeguards
Responsible parties must secure the integrity and confidentiality of personal
information by taking appropriate, reasonable technical and organisational measures.
Failure to implement adequate security safeguards resulting in a compromise of
personal information is a serious violation of POPIA.

3. Notification of Security Compromises
Where there are reasonable grounds to believe that personal information has been
accessed or acquired by an unauthorised person, the Information Regulator and the
data subject must be notified as soon as reasonably possible. Failure to notify is a
violation subject to enforcement action.

4. Direct Marketing
Personal information may not be used for direct marketing by electronic means
without the data subject's consent. Sending direct marketing communications without
consent is a violation of POPIA and subject to enforcement action by the Regulator.

5. Transborder Information Flows
Personal information may not be transferred to a foreign country unless that country
provides an adequate level of protection or the data subject consents. Unlawful
cross-border transfers are subject to enforcement action and penalties.
