DATA MINIMISATION AND PURPOSE LIMITATION POLICY
Based on: GDPR Article 5(1)(b) and (c), UK GDPR, and ICO Accountability Guidance

1. Purpose Specification
The purposes for which personal data is collected must be specified, explicit, and
legitimate at the time of collection. Processing personal data for purposes not
disclosed to individuals at the time of collection is a violation of purpose
limitation and subject to supervisory authority enforcement and fines.

2. Compatible Use Assessment
Where personal data is to be used for a new purpose, a compatibility assessment must
be documented. Processing for an incompatible new purpose without a fresh legal basis
or consent is unlawful under GDPR Article 5(1)(b).

3. Data Minimisation
Only personal data that is adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary in
relation to the purposes of processing may be collected. Excessive data collection
is a violation of the data minimisation principle subject to ICO investigation
and enforcement action.

4. Anonymisation and Pseudonymisation
Where personal data is retained for statistical, research, or archival purposes,
organisations should implement anonymisation or pseudonymisation to minimise privacy
risks. Failure to implement available privacy-enhancing techniques may be an
aggravating factor in the event of a data breach.

5. Privacy Impact on Marketing and Analytics
Use of personal data for profiling, behavioural advertising, or analytics must be
limited to data that is necessary for the specific activity. Obtaining data for one
purpose and repurposing it for marketing without consent or a compatible use
assessment is a violation of both purpose limitation and data minimisation principles.
