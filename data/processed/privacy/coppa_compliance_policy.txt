CHILDREN'S ONLINE PRIVACY PROTECTION POLICY
Based on: Children's Online Privacy Protection Rule, 16 CFR Part 312 (FTC, amended 2025)

1. Scope of Application
This policy applies to operators of websites and online services directed to children
under 13 years of age, and to operators with actual knowledge that they collect
personal information from children under 13. Operators who fail to comply with COPPA
are subject to civil penalties of up to $51,744 per violation.

2. Verifiable Parental Consent
Operators must obtain verifiable parental consent before collecting, using, or
disclosing personal information from children. Collecting personal information from
children without verifiable parental consent is a violation of the COPPA Rule and
subject to FTC enforcement action and civil penalties.

3. Privacy Notice Requirements
Operators must post a clear and prominent privacy notice on their website describing
what information is collected from children, how it is used, and disclosure practices.
Failure to provide a compliant privacy notice is an independent COPPA violation.

4. Data Retention and Deletion
Personal information collected from children must be retained only as long as
reasonably necessary to fulfil the purpose for which it was collected. Upon a parent's
request, operators must delete all personal information about a child. Failure to
delete upon valid request constitutes a violation.

5. Prohibition on Behavioural Advertising
Operators may not condition a child's participation in an activity on the collection
of more personal information than is reasonably necessary for that activity.
Conditioning access to services on excessive data collection is an unfair practice
under COPPA and the FTC Act.
