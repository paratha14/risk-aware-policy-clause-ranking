FERPA — STUDENT EDUCATIONAL RECORDS PRIVACY POLICY
Based on: Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act, 20 U.S.C. § 1232g; 34 CFR Part 99

1. Access Rights
Students who are 18 or older (or enrolled in postsecondary education) have the right
to inspect and review their education records within 45 days of a request. Denial of
access without lawful grounds constitutes a FERPA violation. Institutions that
violate FERPA may lose federal funding administered by the Department of Education.

2. Consent for Disclosure
Educational institutions must obtain written consent before disclosing education
records to third parties. Disclosure without consent is prohibited except in specific
circumstances permitted by FERPA. Unlawful disclosure constitutes a violation
subject to complaint, investigation, and potential loss of federal funding.

3. Directory Information
Institutions may disclose designated directory information without consent only after
notifying students and providing an opportunity to opt out. Disclosure of information
that a student has opted out of having disclosed constitutes a FERPA violation.

4. School Officials Exception
Education records may be shared with school officials who have a legitimate
educational interest without student consent. Officials must have a genuine need to
review the record. Disclosure to officials without a legitimate educational interest
is an unlawful disclosure under FERPA.

5. Complaint and Enforcement
Students or parents may file complaints with the Student Privacy Policy Office of the
U.S. Department of Education. The Department may require corrective action. Continued
non-compliance following a finding of violation may result in termination of federal
funding to the institution.
