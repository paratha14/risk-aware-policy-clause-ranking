UK GDPR DATA PROTECTION COMPLIANCE POLICY
Based on: UK General Data Protection Regulation and Data Protection Act 2018

1. Lawful Basis for Processing
All processing of personal data must have a lawful basis under Article 6 UK GDPR.
Processing without a lawful basis is unlawful. The ICO may issue enforcement notices,
assessment notices, and fines of up to £17.5 million or 4% of global annual turnover,
whichever is higher, for serious violations.

2. Data Subject Rights
Data subjects have the right to access, rectify, erase, restrict, and port their
personal data. Controllers must respond to requests without undue delay and within
one calendar month. Failure to respond within the required timeframe is a violation
subject to ICO investigation and enforcement action.

3. Data Protection by Design
Controllers must implement data protection by design and by default. This requires
appropriate technical and organisational measures from the outset of any processing
activity. Failure to implement data protection by design is a violation subject to
reprimand and fines.

4. Data Protection Impact Assessments
A DPIA is mandatory before undertaking processing likely to result in high risk to
individuals. Failure to conduct a required DPIA is a violation. Where a DPIA indicates
high residual risk, the ICO must be consulted before processing commences.

5. International Transfers
Personal data must not be transferred to a country outside the UK unless that country
ensures an adequate level of data protection or appropriate safeguards are in place.
Unlawful international transfers are subject to enforcement action and fines.
