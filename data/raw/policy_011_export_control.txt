U.S. EXPORT CONTROL COMPLIANCE POLICY

Regulation: ITAR, EAR, OFAC
Classification Level: Restricted
Effective Date: January 1, 2025

1. POLICY STATEMENT

1.1 This organization complies with all U.S. export control laws including the Export Administration Regulations (EAR), International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR), and Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) sanctions.

1.2 All employees involved in international transactions must comply with this policy.

1.3 Violations of export control laws may result in criminal penalties including imprisonment up to 20 years and fines up to $1,000,000 for individuals.

2. PROHIBITED DESTINATIONS

2.1 No goods, technology, or technical data may be exported to embargoed countries without specific license approval.

2.2 Current embargoed destinations include: Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Syria, and regions of Ukraine under Russian control.

2.3 Exporting to prohibited destinations without authorization is a criminal offense subject to severe penalties.

3. DENIED PARTIES SCREENING

3.1 All international transactions must be screened against denied parties lists before proceeding.

3.2 Screening must include Denied Persons List, Entity List, Specially Designated Nationals List, and Unverified List.

3.3 Transactions with parties on denied lists are strictly prohibited.

3.4 Failure to screen may result in transaction prohibition and employee sanctions.

4. EXPORT CLASSIFICATION

4.1 All items must be classified according to the Commerce Control List (CCL) or U.S. Munitions List (USML) before export.

4.2 Items controlled for national security, weapons proliferation, or regional stability reasons require export licenses.

4.3 Exporting controlled items without proper classification and licensing may result in criminal prosecution.

5. LICENSE REQUIREMENTS

5.1 Employees must not ship items requiring export licenses without obtaining proper authorization.

5.2 License applications must include accurate and complete information.

5.3 Making false statements on license applications is a criminal offense.

5.4 Operating under license exceptions requires strict compliance with exception terms.

6. TECHNICAL DATA TRANSFERS

6.1 Technical data and technology transfers are subject to export controls even when no physical goods are exported.

6.2 Sharing controlled technical data with foreign nationals, even within the United States, may constitute a "deemed export" requiring authorization.

6.3 Unauthorized disclosure of controlled technical data may result in termination and criminal charges.

7. FOREIGN NATIONAL EMPLOYEES

7.1 Employment of foreign nationals in positions with access to controlled technology requires evaluation under deemed export rules.

7.2 Technology Control Plans may be required for foreign national employees.

7.3 Failure to comply with deemed export requirements may result in violations.

8. RECORD KEEPING

8.1 Export records must be maintained for five years (EAR) or five years from date of shipment (ITAR).

8.2 Records must be readily accessible for government inspection.

8.3 Failure to maintain adequate records may result in penalties even without other violations.

9. RED FLAGS

9.1 Employees must be aware of red flag indicators of potential illegal exports.

9.2 Red flags include: customer evasive about end use, shipping route abnormal, customer unfamiliar with product, payment from unrelated third party.

9.3 Employees who observe red flags must report to Export Compliance Officer immediately.

9.4 Proceeding with suspicious transactions without investigation may result in violations.

10. TRAINING REQUIREMENTS

10.1 Employees involved in export transactions must complete annual export compliance training.

10.2 Training completion must be documented.

10.3 Employees who fail to complete training may not participate in export activities.

11. SANCTIONS FOR VIOLATIONS

11.1 Administrative penalties: Up to $300,000 per violation (EAR) or $1,094,010 per violation (ITAR).

11.2 Criminal penalties: Up to $1,000,000 per violation and/or 20 years imprisonment.

11.3 Denial of export privileges: Government may prohibit individuals or companies from participating in export activities.

11.4 Internal sanctions: Violations result in disciplinary action up to and including immediate termination.

12. VOLUNTARY SELF-DISCLOSURE

12.1 Suspected violations must be reported to Export Compliance Officer immediately.

12.2 Company may submit Voluntary Self-Disclosure to government which may result in reduced penalties.

12.3 Concealing violations may result in enhanced penalties.

13. AUDITS AND INVESTIGATIONS

13.1 Export transactions are subject to periodic internal audits.

13.2 Government agencies may conduct inspections without prior notice.

13.3 Employees must cooperate fully with investigations.

13.4 Obstruction of investigations may result in additional violations.

14. EXCEPTIONS TO POLICY

No exceptions to this policy are permitted. All export transactions must comply with applicable laws.
