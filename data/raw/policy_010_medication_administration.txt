MEDICATION ADMINISTRATION AND MANAGEMENT POLICY

Policy Number: MED-ADM-2024
Revision: 8
Date: February 1, 2025

1. PURPOSE

This policy establishes procedures for safe medication administration and management to prevent medication errors and ensure patient safety.

2. AUTHORIZED PERSONNEL

2.1 Only licensed nurses, physicians, and authorized personnel may administer medications.

2.2 Unlicensed personnel may not administer medications except as specifically permitted by state law and facility policy.

2.3 Administering medications without proper authorization is a criminal offense and will result in immediate termination and report to licensing board.

3. FIVE RIGHTS OF MEDICATION ADMINISTRATION

3.1 Right Patient: Verify patient identity using two identifiers before administering any medication.

3.2 Right Medication: Verify the medication against the order.

3.3 Right Dose: Verify the dose is correct and within safe range.

3.4 Right Route: Verify the route of administration.

3.5 Right Time: Administer medications within 30 minutes before or after scheduled time.

3.6 Failure to verify any of the five rights may result in patient harm and disciplinary action including license revocation.

4. MEDICATION ORDERS

4.1 Verbal orders are permitted only in emergency situations.

4.2 Verbal orders must be documented immediately and co-signed by ordering physician within 24 hours.

4.3 Unsigned verbal orders older than 24 hours must not be administered without new order.

4.4 Do not accept verbal orders for high-alert medications except in life-threatening emergencies.

5. CONTROLLED SUBSTANCES

5.1 Controlled substances must be stored in double-locked secure areas.

5.2 Count of controlled substances must be verified at each shift change.

5.3 Discrepancies in controlled substance counts must be reported immediately to pharmacy and security.

5.4 Diversion of controlled substances is a criminal offense resulting in termination, license revocation, and criminal prosecution.

6. HIGH-ALERT MEDICATIONS

6.1 High-alert medications (insulin, heparin, warfarin, chemotherapy agents) require independent double-check before administration.

6.2 Bypassing double-check requirements is a serious violation that may result in patient death and staff termination.

6.3 High-alert medication errors must be reported immediately to pharmacy and physician.

7. MEDICATION ERRORS

7.1 Any deviation from the order, even if no harm occurred, must be reported immediately.

7.2 Near misses must also be reported to prevent future errors.

7.3 Failure to report medication errors is a serious violation and may result in patient harm and staff sanctions.

7.4 Staff who report errors in good faith will not be subject to punitive action (Just Culture).

8. MEDICATION STORAGE

8.1 Medications must be stored according to manufacturer instructions (temperature, light exposure).

8.2 Expired medications must be removed from stock immediately and must not be administered.

8.3 Administering expired medication is a violation that may cause patient harm.

8.4 Look-alike, sound-alike medications must be stored separately with warnings.

9. PATIENT EDUCATION

9.1 Patients must be educated about medications including purpose, side effects, and precautions.

9.2 Patients have the right to refuse medications.

9.3 Medication refusals must be documented and physician notified.

10. DOCUMENTATION

10.1 Medications must be documented immediately after administration, not before.

10.2 Pre-charting medications is strictly prohibited and may result in termination.

10.3 Documentation must include date, time, dose, route, site, and patient response.

10.4 Falsifying medication administration records is grounds for immediate termination and license revocation.

11. ADVERSE REACTIONS

11.1 Adverse drug reactions must be reported to physician immediately.

11.2 Serious adverse reactions must be reported to FDA MedWatch.

11.3 Patients experiencing allergic reactions require immediate intervention and must not receive the medication again without physician order.

12. IV MEDICATION ADMINISTRATION

12.1 IV medications must be prepared in sterile environment following aseptic technique.

12.2 IV push medications require specific training and competency verification.

12.3 Incompatible medications must not be mixed or administered through same IV line.

12.4 IV site must be assessed before each medication administration.

13. PEDIATRIC CONSIDERATIONS

13.1 Pediatric doses must be calculated based on weight in kilograms and verified by second nurse.

13.2 Adult doses must never be administered to pediatric patients without weight-based calculation.

13.3 Parents/guardians must be present during medication administration when possible.

14. PENALTIES FOR VIOLATIONS

14.1 Minor errors without patient harm: Counseling and retraining.

14.2 Moderate errors: Written warning and competency verification.

14.3 Serious errors causing patient harm: Suspension pending investigation.

14.4 Gross negligence or willful misconduct: Immediate termination and report to state board of nursing.
