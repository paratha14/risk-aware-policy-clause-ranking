INFECTION PREVENTION AND CONTROL POLICY

Policy ID: IPC-2024-001
Revision: 7.0
Date Effective: January 1, 2025

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

1.1 This policy establishes procedures to prevent transmission of infectious agents in the healthcare setting.

1.2 All healthcare workers, including physicians, nurses, therapists, technicians, students, volunteers, and contractors, must comply with this policy.

1.3 Non-compliance with infection control procedures may result in patient harm and staff disciplinary action.

2. STANDARD PRECAUTIONS

2.1 Standard precautions apply to all patients regardless of known infection status.

2.2 Hand hygiene must be performed before and after every patient contact using soap and water or alcohol-based hand rub.

2.3 Failure to perform hand hygiene is a violation and may result in written warning.

2.4 Gloves must be worn for contact with blood, body fluids, mucous membranes, non-intact skin, and contaminated items.

2.5 Gowns must be worn to protect skin and clothing during procedures likely to generate splash.

2.6 Masks and eye protection must be worn during procedures likely to generate spray.

3. TRANSMISSION-BASED PRECAUTIONS

3.1 Contact Precautions: Required for infections spread by direct or indirect contact (e.g., C. difficile, MRSA, VRE). Patients must be placed in private room or cohorted. Dedicated equipment required.

3.2 Droplet Precautions: Required for infections spread by respiratory droplets (e.g., influenza, pertussis). Surgical mask required within 6 feet of patient.

3.3 Airborne Precautions: Required for infections spread by airborne particles (e.g., tuberculosis, measles, varicella). Patient must be in airborne infection isolation room. N95 respirator required. Fit testing must be current.

3.4 Entering isolation room without required PPE is a serious violation that may result in suspension.

4. HAND HYGIENE

4.1 Hand hygiene is the single most important measure to prevent transmission.

4.2 Alcohol-based hand rub is preferred unless hands are visibly soiled.

4.3 Soap and water required after caring for patients with C. difficile or norovirus.

4.4 Hand hygiene compliance is monitored. Compliance rate below 80% triggers intervention.

5. PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT

5.1 PPE must be selected based on anticipated exposure.

5.2 PPE must be donned before entering patient room and doffed before exiting.

5.3 Gloves must be changed between patients and between dirty and clean tasks on same patient.

5.4 Contaminated PPE must not be worn outside patient room.

6. RESPIRATORY HYGIENE/COUGH ETIQUETTE

6.1 Cover nose and mouth with tissue when coughing or sneezing.

6.2 Dispose of tissues in waste receptacles immediately.

6.3 Perform hand hygiene after contact with respiratory secretions.

6.4 Symptomatic staff must wear surgical mask or stay home.

7. SAFE INJECTION PRACTICES

7.1 Needles and syringes are single-use items and must never be reused for another patient.

7.2 Medication vials must be accessed with new needle and syringe each time.

7.3 Reusing needles or syringes on multiple patients is a criminal offense and will result in immediate termination, license revocation, and criminal prosecution.

7.4 Single-dose vials must not be used for multiple patients.

8. SHARPS SAFETY

8.1 Needles must not be recapped using two-hand technique.

8.2 Sharps must be disposed in sharps containers immediately after use.

8.3 Sharps containers must not be overfilled. Replace when 3/4 full.

8.4 Needlestick injuries must be reported immediately to Employee Health.

9. ENVIRONMENTAL CLEANING

9.1 Patient care areas must be cleaned daily and when soiled.

9.2 High-touch surfaces must be cleaned more frequently.

9.3 Discharge cleaning must be performed between patients using EPA-registered disinfectant.

9.4 Equipment must be cleaned and disinfected between patients.

10. LINEN HANDLING

10.1 Contaminated linen must be handled carefully to avoid airborne contamination.

10.2 Contaminated linen must not be sorted in patient care areas.

10.3 Contaminated linen must be placed in appropriate bags at point of use.

11. WASTE MANAGEMENT

11.1 Regulated medical waste must be segregated from general waste.

11.2 Sharps, cultures, pathology specimens, blood products, and saturated items are regulated medical waste.

11.3 Waste must be placed in appropriate containers following color-coding system.

12. OCCUPATIONAL HEALTH REQUIREMENTS

12.1 Healthcare workers must have documented immunity to measles, mumps, rubella, varicella, and hepatitis B.

12.2 Annual tuberculosis screening is required.

12.3 Annual influenza vaccination is required unless medical contraindication documented.

12.4 Staff who decline influenza vaccination must wear mask during influenza season when in patient care areas.

12.5 Staff with infectious conditions (e.g., active tuberculosis, chickenpox, measles, influenza) must be excluded from work until no longer infectious.

13. OUTBREAK MANAGEMENT

13.1 Clusters of infections must be reported to Infection Prevention immediately.

13.2 Outbreak investigations will be conducted to identify source and control transmission.

13.3 Additional control measures may be implemented during outbreaks.

14. VISITOR RESTRICTIONS

14.1 Visitors with symptoms of infection should be discouraged from visiting.

14.2 Visitors may be required to wear PPE when visiting patients on precautions.

14.3 Visitor restrictions may be implemented during outbreaks.

15. CONSTRUCTION AND RENOVATION

15.1 Infection Control Risk Assessment must be completed before construction.

15.2 Barriers and negative pressure required for demolition in occupied areas.

15.3 Construction workers must follow facility infection control requirements.

16. PENALTIES FOR VIOLATIONS

16.1 First violation: Verbal counseling and education.

16.2 Second violation: Written warning.

16.3 Third violation: Mandatory skills competency verification and possible suspension.

16.4 Serious violations endangering patients: Immediate suspension pending investigation.

16.5 Criminal violations (e.g., needle reuse): Termination and report to licensing board and law enforcement.
