WORKPLACE HEALTH AND SAFETY POLICY

Document ID: WHS-2024-v6
Issue Date: January 10, 2025

ARTICLE 1: POLICY STATEMENT

1.1 The Company is committed to providing a safe and healthy workplace for all employees.

1.2 Compliance with all applicable safety regulations is mandatory.

1.3 Safety violations that endanger others may result in immediate termination.

ARTICLE 2: EMPLOYEE OBLIGATIONS

2.1 Employees must follow all safety procedures and use required personal protective equipment (PPE).

2.2 Failure to wear required PPE results in written warning for first offense.

2.3 Second PPE violation within 12 months results in 3-day suspension without pay.

2.4 Third violation results in termination.

2.5 Employees must report all workplace hazards and injuries immediately to supervisors.

ARTICLE 3: HAZARD IDENTIFICATION AND REPORTING

3.1 Employees who identify hazards must report them immediately using the hazard reporting system.

3.2 Supervisors must investigate reported hazards within 24 hours.

3.3 Failure to investigate hazards may result in supervisor disciplinary action.

3.4 Employees who fail to report known hazards that result in injury may be held liable.

ARTICLE 4: ACCIDENT AND INJURY REPORTING

4.1 All workplace accidents and injuries must be reported to HR within 2 hours of occurrence.

4.2 Failure to report injuries within 2 hours may result in denial of workers' compensation benefits.

4.3 Supervisors must complete incident investigation reports within 24 hours.

4.4 Falsifying accident reports is grounds for immediate termination and possible criminal prosecution.

ARTICLE 5: SAFETY TRAINING REQUIREMENTS

5.1 All employees must complete general safety orientation before beginning work.

5.2 Job-specific safety training must be completed within first week of assignment.

5.3 Annual safety refresher training is mandatory for all employees.

5.4 Employees who fail to complete training by deadline will be removed from work until training is completed, without pay.

ARTICLE 6: HAZARDOUS MATERIALS

6.1 Only trained and authorized personnel may handle hazardous materials.

6.2 Safety Data Sheets (SDS) must be consulted before using any hazardous substance.

6.3 Improper handling or disposal of hazardous materials may result in termination and criminal charges.

6.4 Chemical spills must be reported immediately. Attempting to clean spills without proper training is prohibited.

ARTICLE 7: MACHINE GUARDING AND LOCKOUT/TAGOUT

7.1 Machine guards must never be disabled or removed except during authorized maintenance.

7.2 Removing safety guards without authorization results in immediate termination.

7.3 Lockout/Tagout procedures must be followed for all maintenance and service activities.

7.4 Failure to follow lockout/tagout procedures may result in serious injury or death and will result in immediate termination.

ARTICLE 8: ERGONOMICS AND REPETITIVE STRAIN

8.1 Employees must use proper lifting techniques and seek assistance for loads over 50 pounds.

8.2 Workstation ergonomics assessments are available upon request.

8.3 Employees experiencing discomfort or pain must report it immediately to prevent injury.

ARTICLE 9: WORKPLACE VIOLENCE PREVENTION

9.1 Threats of violence are taken seriously and result in immediate investigation and potential termination.

9.2 Physical violence results in immediate termination and criminal prosecution.

9.3 Weapons are strictly prohibited on Company premises. Violation results in immediate termination and notification to law enforcement.

ARTICLE 10: DRUG AND ALCOHOL POLICY

10.1 Working under the influence of drugs or alcohol is strictly prohibited and results in immediate termination.

10.2 The Company reserves the right to conduct random drug testing.

10.3 Refusal to submit to drug testing when requested results in termination.

10.4 Employees taking prescription medications that may impair ability to work safely must notify their supervisor.

ARTICLE 11: EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

11.1 All employees must know evacuation routes and assembly points.

11.2 Employees must participate in emergency drills when scheduled.

11.3 Interfering with emergency drills or false alarms may result in disciplinary action.

11.4 During evacuations, employees must leave immediately without stopping to collect personal belongings.

ARTICLE 12: CONTRACTOR SAFETY

12.1 Contractors must comply with all Company safety policies while on premises.

12.2 Company employees must not authorize contractors to perform work if safety requirements are not met.

12.3 Contractors who violate safety policies will be removed from the premises and contract may be terminated.

ARTICLE 13: PENALTIES FOR VIOLATIONS

13.1 Minor violations: Verbal warning and counseling.

13.2 Moderate violations: Written warning and mandatory retraining.

13.3 Serious violations: Suspension or termination depending on severity.

13.4 Critical violations endangering life: Immediate termination without prior warning.

ARTICLE 14: RIGHT TO REFUSE UNSAFE WORK

14.1 Employees have the right to refuse work they believe is unsafe without retaliation.

14.2 Such concerns must be reported to supervision immediately for investigation.

14.3 Retaliation against employees who raise safety concerns is prohibited and will result in disciplinary action against the retaliator.
