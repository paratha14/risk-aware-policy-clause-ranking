GENERAL DATA PROTECTION REGULATION (GDPR) COMPLIANCE POLICY

Document Reference: GDPR-POL-2025
Version: 4.0
Effective: January 1, 2025

ARTICLE 1: SCOPE

1.1 This policy applies to all processing of personal data of individuals in the European Union.

1.2 GDPR applies regardless of whether the processing takes place in the EU.

1.3 All employees handling EU personal data must comply with this policy.

ARTICLE 2: LAWFUL BASIS FOR PROCESSING

2.1 Personal data may only be processed under one of six lawful bases: consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, or legitimate interests.

2.2 Consent must be freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous.

2.3 Consent must be as easy to withdraw as to give.

2.4 Pre-ticked boxes or silence do not constitute valid consent.

2.5 Processing without lawful basis is a serious violation subject to fines up to €20 million or 4% of annual global revenue, whichever is higher.

ARTICLE 3: DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS

3.1 Right of Access: Data subjects may request copy of their personal data. Requests must be fulfilled within one month.

3.2 Right to Rectification: Data subjects may request correction of inaccurate data. Corrections must be made without undue delay.

3.3 Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"): Data subjects may request deletion of their data. Requests must be evaluated and honored unless exception applies.

3.4 Right to Restriction: Data subjects may request processing be limited in certain circumstances.

3.5 Right to Data Portability: Data subjects may request their data in machine-readable format.

3.6 Right to Object: Data subjects may object to processing, particularly for direct marketing.

3.7 Automated Decision Making: Data subjects have right not to be subject to solely automated decisions with legal effects.

3.8 Failure to honor data subject rights may result in supervisory authority enforcement action and significant fines.

ARTICLE 4: DATA PROTECTION PRINCIPLES

4.1 Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency: Processing must be legal, fair, and transparent to data subjects.

4.2 Purpose Limitation: Data collected for specific purposes must not be used for incompatible purposes.

4.3 Data Minimization: Only collect data that is necessary for the stated purpose.

4.4 Accuracy: Data must be accurate and kept up to date.

4.5 Storage Limitation: Data must not be kept longer than necessary.

4.6 Integrity and Confidentiality: Data must be processed securely.

4.7 Accountability: Controller must demonstrate compliance.

ARTICLE 5: SPECIAL CATEGORIES OF PERSONAL DATA

5.1 Processing of special categories (racial/ethnic origin, political opinions, religious beliefs, trade union membership, genetic data, biometric data, health data, sex life, or sexual orientation) is prohibited unless specific condition is met.

5.2 Explicit consent is required for processing special category data in most cases.

5.3 Enhanced safeguards are required when processing special category data.

5.4 Unauthorized processing of special category data may result in maximum penalties.

ARTICLE 6: CHILDREN'S DATA

6.1 Processing children's data (under age 16 or lower if member state permits) based on consent requires parental consent.

6.2 Reasonable efforts must be made to verify parental consent.

6.3 Children's data requires enhanced protection.

ARTICLE 7: DATA BREACH NOTIFICATION

7.1 Personal data breaches must be reported to relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours unless breach is unlikely to result in risk to individuals.

7.2 Data subjects must be notified without undue delay if breach is likely to result in high risk to their rights and freedoms.

7.3 Failure to report breaches within 72 hours may result in additional penalties.

7.4 Breach records must be maintained documenting facts, effects, and remedial action taken.

ARTICLE 8: DATA PROTECTION IMPACT ASSESSMENTS

8.1 DPIAs are required for processing likely to result in high risk, particularly when using new technologies.

8.2 DPIAs must be completed before commencing processing.

8.3 DPIAs must identify risks and mitigation measures.

8.4 Supervisory authority must be consulted if DPIA indicates high risk that cannot be mitigated.

ARTICLE 9: DATA PROTECTION OFFICER

9.1 A Data Protection Officer (DPO) must be appointed for certain categories of controllers and processors.

9.2 DPO must be independent and report to highest management level.

9.3 DPO contact information must be published and communicated to supervisory authority.

9.4 DPO must be consulted on all data protection matters.

ARTICLE 10: INTERNATIONAL DATA TRANSFERS

10.1 Personal data may only be transferred to third countries with adequate protection level.

10.2 Adequacy decisions, Standard Contractual Clauses, Binding Corporate Rules, or other approved mechanisms must be used.

10.3 Transfers without appropriate safeguards are prohibited and subject to severe penalties.

10.4 Additional safeguards may be required depending on third country laws.

ARTICLE 11: PROCESSOR OBLIGATIONS

11.1 Processors may only process personal data on documented instructions from controller.

11.2 Processors must maintain record of processing activities.

11.3 Processors must implement appropriate security measures.

11.4 Processors must notify controller of data breaches without undue delay.

11.5 Processors must assist controller in fulfilling data subject rights requests.

ARTICLE 12: RECORDS OF PROCESSING ACTIVITIES

12.1 Organizations with 250+ employees must maintain records of all processing activities.

12.2 Smaller organizations must maintain records if processing is regular, likely to result in risk, or involves special categories.

12.3 Records must include purposes, categories of data, recipients, transfers, retention periods, and security measures.

ARTICLE 13: PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE

13.1 Tier 1 Violations (administrative requirements): Up to €10 million or 2% of annual global revenue.

13.2 Tier 2 Violations (data protection principles, data subject rights): Up to €20 million or 4% of annual global revenue.

13.3 Penalties are cumulative and may be imposed for each violation.

13.4 Supervisory authorities may also impose processing bans, warnings, and orders to comply.

ARTICLE 14: EMPLOYEE OBLIGATIONS

Employees who violate this policy may face disciplinary action up to and including termination, in addition to potential personal liability for certain violations.
