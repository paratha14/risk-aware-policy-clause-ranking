ENVIRONMENTAL COMPLIANCE POLICY

Document ID: ENV-COMP-2024
Classification: Controlled
Effective Date: January 1, 2025

SECTION 1: POLICY STATEMENT

1.1 The Company is committed to environmental protection and compliance with all applicable environmental laws.

1.2 All employees must comply with environmental requirements relevant to their work.

1.3 Environmental violations may result in significant fines, criminal prosecution, and reputational damage.

SECTION 2: APPLICABLE REGULATIONS

2.1 This policy addresses compliance with Clean Air Act, Clean Water Act, Resource Conservation and Recovery Act, Comprehensive Environmental Response Compensation and Liability Act, Toxic Substances Control Act, and state/local environmental laws.

2.2 Facility-specific environmental permits must be posted and compliance maintained.

2.3 Permit violations must be reported to regulatory agencies per permit terms and may result in permit revocation.

SECTION 3: AIR EMISSIONS

3.1 Air emissions from processes must be controlled per permit requirements.

3.2 Emissions monitoring equipment must be maintained and calibrated per schedule.

3.3 Excess emissions or monitoring malfunctions must be reported to air quality agency within 24 hours.

3.4 Opacity readings exceeding permit limits must be corrected immediately.

3.5 Tampering with emissions controls is a criminal violation.

SECTION 4: WASTEWATER DISCHARGES

4.1 Wastewater discharged to sewer or surface water must meet permit limits.

4.2 Wastewater samples must be collected and analyzed per permit schedule.

4.3 Sample results exceeding permit limits must be reported to water authority within 24 hours.

4.4 Bypass of treatment systems is prohibited except in emergencies and must be reported immediately.

4.5 Discharge monitoring reports must be submitted monthly by the 28th.

SECTION 5: HAZARDOUS WASTE MANAGEMENT

5.1 Hazardous wastes must be accumulated in appropriate containers properly labeled with hazardous waste labels.

5.2 Satellite accumulation areas must not exceed 55 gallons per waste stream.

5.3 Containers must be inspected weekly for leaks and damage.

5.4 Hazardous waste may only be shipped to EPA-permitted disposal facilities.

5.5 Waste manifests must be completed accurately and signed.

5.6 Failure to receive signed manifest copy within 35 days triggers exception report to EPA.

5.7 Illegal disposal of hazardous waste may result in fines up to $50,000 per day and imprisonment up to 5 years.

SECTION 6: SPILL PREVENTION AND RESPONSE

6.1 Spills of hazardous substances exceeding reportable quantities must be reported to National Response Center immediately.

6.2 Oil spills to navigable waters must be reported immediately regardless of quantity.

6.3 Spill response equipment must be available and personnel trained.

6.4 Spill Prevention Control and Countermeasure Plans must be followed.

6.5 Secondary containment is required for oil storage exceeding 1,320 gallons.

6.6 Failure to report spills may result in penalties up to $50,000 per day.

SECTION 7: UNDERGROUND STORAGE TANKS

7.1 USTs must be registered with state agency.

7.2 Leak detection must be performed monthly.

7.3 Corrosion protection systems must be tested per schedule.

7.4 Tank tightness testing required for older tanks.

7.5 Suspected releases must be reported to state within 24 hours.

7.6 Confirmed releases trigger corrective action requirements.

SECTION 8: CHEMICAL REPORTING

8.1 Tier II Chemical Inventory Reports due March 1st annually.

8.2 Toxic Release Inventory Reports due July 1st annually for listed chemicals above thresholds.

8.3 Reports must be accurate and complete. Knowingly false reports are criminal violations.

8.4 Environmental Compliance Manager responsible for ensuring timely submission.

SECTION 9: STORMWATER MANAGEMENT

9.1 Industrial facilities require Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plans.

9.2 Stormwater BMPs must be implemented and maintained.

9.3 Quarterly facility inspections required.

9.4 Discharge monitoring required per permit.

9.5 Contaminated stormwater must not be discharged to storm drains.

SECTION 10: ASBESTOS AND LEAD PAINT

10.1 Asbestos-containing materials must not be disturbed without proper notification and licensed contractor.

10.2 Demolition or renovation of facilities built before 1980 requires asbestos survey.

10.3 Asbestos notifications must be submitted 10 business days before work begins.

10.4 Lead paint from pre-1978 buildings requires special handling and disposal.

SECTION 11: TRAINING REQUIREMENTS

11.1 Hazardous waste handlers must complete annual training.

11.2 Spill responders must complete 24-hour HAZWOPER training.

11.3 Training records must be maintained for 3 years.

SECTION 12: RECORD RETENTION

12.1 Environmental permits and applications: Permanent.

12.2 Monitoring data and reports: Minimum 5 years.

12.3 Hazardous waste manifests: 3 years.

12.4 Training records: 3 years from termination.

SECTION 13: ENVIRONMENTAL AUDITS

13.1 Facilities will be audited annually for compliance.

13.2 Audit findings must be corrected within specified timeframes.

13.3 Failure to correct audit findings may result in regulatory violation.

SECTION 14: CONSEQUENCES

14.1 Employee violations: Disciplinary action up to termination.

14.2 Regulatory violations: Civil penalties up to $50,000 per day.

14.3 Criminal violations: Fines up to $250,000 for individuals and imprisonment up to 15 years.

14.4 Knowing endangerment violations: Fines up to $1,000,000 and imprisonment up to 15 years.
