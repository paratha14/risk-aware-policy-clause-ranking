EMPLOYEE SOCIAL MEDIA POLICY

Policy Number: HR-SM-2024
Version: 2.0
Effective Date: January 1, 2025

1. PURPOSE

This policy provides guidelines for employee use of social media and protects the Company's reputation and confidential information.

2. SCOPE

2.1 This policy applies to all employees when posting about the Company or job-related matters.

2.2 The policy applies to personal and professional social media accounts.

2.3 The policy applies to social media use both during and outside work hours.

3. PERSONAL SOCIAL MEDIA USE

3.1 Employees are free to use personal social media accounts but must not claim to represent the Company unless authorized.

3.2 Personal opinions expressed on social media should be clearly identified as personal and not Company positions.

3.3 Include disclaimer such as "Views expressed are my own and do not represent my employer."

4. PROHIBITED CONDUCT

4.1 Employees may not disclose confidential or proprietary Company information on social media.

4.2 Employees may not post about pending litigation, investigations, or personnel matters.

4.3 Employees may not harass, bully, or discriminate against coworkers on social media.

4.4 Employees may not post false or defamatory information about the Company, products, or other employees.

4.5 Violations may result in disciplinary action up to termination.

5. CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

5.1 Trade secrets, financial information, strategic plans, and unreleased product information are confidential and may not be shared on social media.

5.2 Inadvertent disclosure of confidential information on social media may still result in disciplinary action.

5.3 Employees who discover they've inadvertently disclosed confidential information must immediately report to their manager and IT to attempt removal.

6. PROFESSIONAL NETWORKING SITES

6.1 Employees should not connect with clients or business partners on personal social media without manager approval.

6.2 Job titles and descriptions on LinkedIn should be accurate and pre-approved.

6.3 Employees leaving the Company must update LinkedIn profiles within 30 days.

7. OFFICIAL COMPANY ACCOUNTS

7.1 Only authorized marketing personnel may post from official Company social media accounts.

7.2 Unauthorized access to Company accounts is a terminable offense.

7.3 All official posts must follow brand guidelines and be approved per marketing protocols.

8. SOCIAL MEDIA DURING WORK HOURS

8.1 Personal social media use during work hours should be limited to breaks and lunch.

8.2 Excessive personal social media use during work hours may result in disciplinary action.

8.3 Company computers and networks may not be used for personal social media without manager approval.

9. RESPONDING TO NEGATIVE COMMENTS

9.1 Employees should not respond to negative comments about the Company without consulting PR department.

9.2 Forward concerns to pr@company.com for coordinated response.

9.3 Do not engage in arguments or debates about the Company online.

10. PROTECTED CONCERTED ACTIVITY

10.1 This policy does not prohibit employees from discussing wages, hours, or working conditions as protected by Section 7 of the National Labor Relations Act.

10.2 Employees may discuss union organizing or workplace concerns on social media.

11. CONSEQUENCES

11.1 Minor violations: Verbal warning and counseling.

11.2 Moderate violations: Written warning and social media training.

11.3 Serious violations: Suspension or termination depending on severity.

11.4 Confidentiality breaches: Immediate termination and potential legal action.

12. MONITORING

The Company may monitor social media for mentions of the Company. However, personal social media accounts will not be systematically monitored.
