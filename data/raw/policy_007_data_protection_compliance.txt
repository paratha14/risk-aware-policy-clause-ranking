CORPORATE DATA PROTECTION AND PRIVACY COMPLIANCE POLICY

Policy Number: DPC-2024-003
Classification: Confidential
Effective Date: January 1, 2025

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

1.1 This policy establishes requirements for protecting personal data and ensuring compliance with applicable privacy laws including GDPR, CCPA, and other regulations.

1.2 This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties handling personal data on behalf of the Company.

1.3 Violation of this policy may result in termination of employment and legal liability.

2. DATA CLASSIFICATION

2.1 Personal Data: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable individual.

2.2 Sensitive Personal Data: Data revealing racial/ethnic origin, political opinions, religious beliefs, health information, biometric data, or genetic data.

2.3 Sensitive data requires enhanced protections and may only be processed under strict legal conditions.

3. LAWFUL BASIS FOR PROCESSING

3.1 Personal data may only be processed when there is a lawful basis such as consent, contract, legal obligation, or legitimate interest.

3.2 Employees must document the lawful basis for each processing activity.

3.3 Processing without lawful basis is a serious violation subject to immediate termination.

4. DATA MINIMIZATION

4.1 Collect only personal data that is necessary for the specified purpose.

4.2 Do not retain personal data longer than necessary for the purpose.

4.3 Excessive data collection without justification may result in disciplinary action.

5. INDIVIDUAL RIGHTS

5.1 Individuals have the right to access their personal data. Requests must be fulfilled within 30 days.

5.2 Failure to respond to access requests within the deadline may result in regulatory fines up to €20 million or 4% of annual revenue.

5.3 Individuals may request deletion of their data. Such requests must be evaluated and fulfilled if no legal basis for retention exists.

5.4 Improperly denying deletion requests may result in enforcement action and personal liability for responsible employees.

6. DATA SECURITY REQUIREMENTS

6.1 All personal data must be encrypted in transit and at rest using approved encryption standards.

6.2 Access to personal data must be restricted to authorized personnel only.

6.3 Multi-factor authentication is required for all systems containing personal data.

6.4 Security breaches must be reported to the Data Protection Officer within 2 hours of discovery.

7. DATA BREACH NOTIFICATION

7.1 Data breaches likely to result in risk to individuals must be reported to supervisory authorities within 72 hours.

7.2 Failure to report within 72 hours may result in additional regulatory fines.

7.3 Individuals must be notified without undue delay if the breach poses high risk to their rights.

7.4 Employees who delay breach notification may face termination and personal legal liability.

8. THIRD-PARTY DATA PROCESSORS

8.1 Third parties processing data on our behalf must sign Data Processing Agreements.

8.2 Due diligence must be performed before engaging any data processor.

8.3 Engaging non-compliant processors may result in Company liability for their violations.

8.4 Employees who engage processors without proper agreements face disciplinary action up to termination.

9. INTERNATIONAL DATA TRANSFERS

9.1 Personal data may only be transferred outside the EU/EEA using approved transfer mechanisms (Standard Contractual Clauses, Adequacy Decisions, or Binding Corporate Rules).

9.2 Unauthorized international transfers are strictly prohibited and may result in termination and criminal prosecution.

9.3 All international transfers must be documented and logged.

10. EMPLOYEE TRAINING AND AWARENESS

10.1 All employees must complete annual data protection training.

10.2 Employees handling sensitive data require specialized training.

10.3 Failure to complete training by the deadline results in system access revocation until training is completed.

11. DATA PROTECTION IMPACT ASSESSMENTS

11.1 DPIAs are required for high-risk processing activities.

11.2 DPIAs must be completed before commencing the processing activity.

11.3 Processing without required DPIA is a policy violation and may violate law.

12. RETENTION AND DELETION

12.1 Personal data must be deleted when no longer needed for the original purpose.

12.2 Retention schedules must be documented for each category of data.

12.3 Retaining data beyond approved schedules without justification is a violation.

13. PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE

13.1 Minor violations: Written warning and mandatory retraining.

13.2 Moderate violations: Suspension without pay and performance improvement plan.

13.3 Serious violations: Immediate termination and potential legal action.

13.4 Willful violations: Termination, legal action, and report to regulatory authorities.

14. REPORTING VIOLATIONS

Employees must report suspected violations to the Data Protection Officer or via the anonymous ethics hotline. Retaliation against whistleblowers is strictly prohibited.
