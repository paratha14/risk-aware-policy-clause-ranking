INSIDER TRADING PREVENTION POLICY

Policy Number: SEC-IT-2024
Classification: Highly Confidential
Effective: January 1, 2025

SECTION 1: PURPOSE

This policy prohibits trading securities while in possession of material non-public information (MNPI) to ensure compliance with federal securities laws.

SECTION 2: APPLICABILITY

2.1 This policy applies to all directors, officers, employees, and contractors of the Company and its subsidiaries.

2.2 The policy applies to trading in Company securities and securities of business partners where employee possesses MNPI.

2.3 The policy applies to trading by family members and controlled entities.

SECTION 3: DEFINITIONS

3.1 Material Information: Information that a reasonable investor would consider important in making investment decisions. This includes financial results, mergers/acquisitions, major contracts, regulatory actions, executive changes, and product launches.

3.2 Non-Public Information: Information not yet released to the public through approved channels. Information becomes public 48 hours after broad public release.

3.3 Insider Trading: Buying or selling securities while in possession of MNPI.

SECTION 4: PROHIBITION ON TRADING

4.1 No person may trade Company securities while in possession of MNPI.

4.2 No person may tip others about MNPI or recommend trading based on MNPI.

4.3 Violations of insider trading laws can result in SEC enforcement actions, criminal prosecution with penalties up to $5 million and 20 years imprisonment, civil penalties up to three times the profit gained or loss avoided, and civil lawsuits from affected parties.

SECTION 5: BLACKOUT PERIODS

5.1 All employees are prohibited from trading during blackout periods beginning on the 15th day of the last month of each fiscal quarter and ending 48 hours after earnings announcement.

5.2 Additional blackout periods may be imposed for specific events such as pending acquisitions.

5.3 Trading during blackout periods, even without knowledge of MNPI, is a policy violation.

5.4 Exceptions for 10b5-1 plans or hardship situations require General Counsel pre-approval.

SECTION 6: PRE-CLEARANCE REQUIREMENTS

6.1 Directors, executive officers, and certain designated employees must pre-clear all trades with the General Counsel.

6.2 Pre-clearance requests must be submitted at least 2 business days before intended trade.

6.3 Pre-clearance approval expires if trade not completed within 2 business days.

6.4 Trading without required pre-clearance is a policy violation even if no MNPI possessed.

SECTION 7: PROHIBITED TRANSACTIONS

7.1 Short Sales: No employee may sell Company securities short. This prohibition applies even if no MNPI possessed.

7.2 Publicly Traded Options: No employee may trade puts, calls, or other derivative securities of the Company.

7.3 Hedging: No employee may hedge their economic risk in Company securities through collars, forwards, or other instruments.

7.4 Pledging: Officers and directors may not pledge Company securities as collateral without Board approval.

7.5 Margin Accounts: Company securities should not be held in margin accounts.

SECTION 8: 10b5-1 TRADING PLANS

8.1 Employees may establish written trading plans meeting SEC Rule 10b5-1 requirements.

8.2 Plans must be established when not in possession of MNPI.

8.3 Cooling-off period of 90 days (directors/officers) or 30 days (others) required before trading begins.

8.4 Plans must specify amounts, prices, or formulas and not permit discretion.

8.5 Plans must be submitted to General Counsel for review and approval before establishment.

SECTION 9: REPORTING OBLIGATIONS

9.1 Section 16 Officers (CEO, CFO, certain VPs) must report trades on SEC Form 4 within 2 business days.

9.2 Late Form 4 filings may result in SEC enforcement action.

9.3 Company assists with Form 4 preparation but timely filing is officer's responsibility.

SECTION 10: POST-TERMINATION TRADING

10.1 This policy continues to apply after termination of employment.

10.2 Former employees who possess MNPI may not trade until information becomes public.

10.3 Pre-clearance requirements cease 6 months after termination for non-officers.

SECTION 11: ENFORCEMENT

11.1 Suspected violations are investigated by General Counsel.

11.2 Confirmed violations result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

11.3 Company will report suspected criminal violations to SEC and DOJ.

11.4 Employees may be required to disgorge profits from violative trades.

SECTION 12: TRAINING

12.1 All employees must complete insider trading training within 30 days of hire.

12.2 Annual refresher training is required.

12.3 Additional training required when assigned to roles with access to MNPI.

SECTION 13: THIRD PARTY CONFIDENTIALITY

13.1 When Company receives confidential information about business partners, employees may not trade in those companies' securities.

13.2 Confidentiality obligations continue after business relationship ends until information becomes public.

SECTION 14: CONSEQUENCES

Insider trading violations are career-ending events. Even appearance of impropriety can damage reputation. When in doubt, do not trade.
