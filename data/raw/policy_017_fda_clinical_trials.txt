FDA CLINICAL TRIAL REGULATIONS COMPLIANCE POLICY

Regulation: 21 CFR Parts 50, 56, 312, 812
Document Version: 2.0
Effective Date: January 1, 2025

SECTION 1: APPLICABILITY

1.1 This policy applies to all clinical investigations of investigational drugs and devices regulated by FDA.

1.2 All investigators, research staff, and sponsors must comply with applicable FDA regulations.

1.3 Violations may result in clinical hold, warning letters, consent decree, debarment, and criminal prosecution.

SECTION 2: INSTITUTIONAL REVIEW BOARD APPROVAL

2.1 No clinical investigation may begin without IRB approval.

2.2 Continuing review must be obtained at intervals appropriate to risk, but not less than annually.

2.3 Conducting research without current IRB approval is a serious violation that will result in immediate investigation suspension and potential criminal charges.

2.4 All protocol amendments must receive IRB approval before implementation unless necessary to eliminate immediate hazard.

SECTION 3: INFORMED CONSENT REQUIREMENTS

3.1 Informed consent must be obtained from all subjects before performing study procedures.

3.2 Consent process must be documented in writing using IRB-approved consent forms.

3.3 Consent forms must include all required elements per 21 CFR 50.25.

3.4 Subjects must be given opportunity to ask questions and adequate time to consider participation.

3.5 No exculpatory language may be included in consent forms.

3.6 Consent may be withdrawn at any time without penalty.

3.7 Enrolling subjects without proper informed consent is a critical violation that may result in study termination and criminal prosecution.

SECTION 4: INVESTIGATIONAL NEW DRUG (IND) REQUIREMENTS

4.1 Investigational drugs may only be used under FDA-approved IND.

4.2 Investigators must follow the protocol without deviations unless necessary for subject safety.

4.3 Significant protocol deviations must be reported to IRB and sponsor.

4.4 Investigational drug must be stored securely under appropriate conditions.

4.5 Drug accountability records must be maintained documenting all dispensing.

4.6 Unused drug must be returned to sponsor or destroyed per sponsor instructions with documentation.

SECTION 5: INVESTIGATIONAL DEVICE EXEMPTION (IDE) REQUIREMENTS

5.1 Significant risk devices require FDA-approved IDE before investigation begins.

5.2 Nonsignificant risk devices require IRB approval but not FDA approval.

5.3 Devices must be labeled "CAUTION - Investigational Device. Limited by Federal Law to Investigational Use."

5.4 Device accountability must be maintained.

5.5 Devices must not be sold or commercialized during investigation.

SECTION 6: ADVERSE EVENT REPORTING

6.1 All adverse events must be documented in source documents and case report forms.

6.2 Serious adverse events must be reported to sponsor immediately (within 24 hours).

6.3 Unanticipated problems involving risk to subjects must be reported to IRB within 5 business days.

6.4 Death of subject must be reported to IRB, sponsor, and FDA (for IND studies) immediately.

6.5 Failure to report serious adverse events is a critical violation and may result in study termination.

SECTION 7: DATA INTEGRITY

7.1 Source documents must be contemporaneous, accurate, complete, and legible.

7.2 All data must be attributable, legible, contemporaneous, original, and accurate (ALCOA principle).

7.3 Corrections must be made by single line through original entry, initialed, dated, with reason documented.

7.4 Data may not be backdated or falsified under any circumstances.

7.5 Falsification of data is research misconduct and a criminal offense that will result in termination, debarment, and criminal prosecution.

SECTION 8: MONITORING AND AUDITS

8.1 Sponsor monitors may visit site to review study conduct and verify data.

8.2 FDA may inspect site without prior notice.

8.3 All study records must be made available for monitoring and inspection.

8.4 Regulatory inspection findings must be responded to promptly with corrective actions.

8.5 Failure to cooperate with inspections may result in regulatory action.

SECTION 9: RECORD RETENTION

9.1 Records must be retained for 2 years after NDA/PMA approval or 2 years after investigation discontinuation.

9.2 Records must be readily available for inspection.

9.3 Essential documents include protocol, consent forms, IRB approvals, correspondence, source documents, and CRFs.

9.4 Destruction of records before end of retention period is violation.

SECTION 10: FINANCIAL DISCLOSURE

10.1 Investigators must disclose financial interests in sponsor to IRB and sponsor.

10.2 Significant financial interests include compensation over $25,000 per year, proprietary interest, or significant equity interest.

10.3 Failure to disclose financial interests is violation that may invalidate study results.

SECTION 11: INVESTIGATOR OBLIGATIONS

11.1 Investigator is responsible for conduct of study and protection of human subjects.

11.2 Investigator must personally conduct or supervise investigation.

11.3 Investigator may not delegate responsibilities to unqualified persons.

11.4 All sub-investigators and research staff must be qualified and trained.

11.5 Inadequate supervision may result in regulatory action against investigator.

SECTION 12: SUBJECT SAFETY

12.1 Subject safety is paramount and takes precedence over protocol compliance.

12.2 Procedures must be taken to minimize risks to subjects.

12.3 Risk to benefit ratio must be favorable and must be continually evaluated.

12.4 Study must be stopped if risks outweigh benefits.

SECTION 13: COMPLIANCE WITH PROTOCOL

13.1 Investigations must be conducted according to signed protocol.

13.2 Protocol deviations must be minimized.

13.3 Eligibility criteria must be strictly followed.

13.4 Frequent protocol violations may result in study suspension.

SECTION 14: PENALTIES FOR VIOLATIONS

14.1 Minor violations: Corrective action required, additional training.

14.2 Moderate violations: Clinical hold, restricted enrollment, increased monitoring.

14.3 Serious violations: Study termination, disqualification of clinical investigator, warning letter.

14.4 Critical violations: Debarment from FDA-regulated research, criminal prosecution, civil penalties.

14.5 FDA may issue Form FDA 483 observations, warning letters, untitled letters, consent decrees, injunctions, or criminal referrals depending on severity.
