STUDENT CODE OF CONDUCT

Policy Code: SCC-2024
Effective Date: Fall 2024 Semester

PART I: JURISDICTION

1.1 This Code applies to all students enrolled at the University.

1.2 The Code applies to conduct on University premises and at University-sponsored events.

1.3 The Code may also apply to off-campus conduct that adversely affects the University community.

PART II: PROHIBITED CONDUCT

2.1 Academic Dishonesty: Any form of cheating, plagiarism, or falsification of academic work is prohibited. First offense results in failing grade; second offense results in expulsion.

2.2 Hazing: Any action that endangers physical or mental health as part of initiation, regardless of consent, is prohibited. Hazing violations result in immediate suspension of organizations and potential criminal charges.

2.3 Alcohol Violations: Underage possession or consumption of alcohol results in $500 fine and mandatory alcohol education. Providing alcohol to minors results in suspension.

2.4 Drug Violations: Possession or distribution of illegal drugs results in immediate suspension pending investigation. Conviction may result in expulsion.

2.5 Theft: Stealing University or personal property results in restitution, criminal charges, and potential expulsion.

2.6 Vandalism: Damaging University property results in restitution, disciplinary probation, and potential suspension.

2.7 Violence: Physical assault results in immediate interim suspension and potential expulsion. Police will be notified.

2.8 Sexual Misconduct: Any form of sexual harassment, assault, or exploitation is strictly prohibited and results in immediate interim measures and potential expulsion. Title IX procedures apply.

2.9 Discrimination: Harassment based on protected characteristics results in disciplinary action up to expulsion.

2.10 Weapons: Possession of firearms or weapons on campus results in immediate expulsion and criminal charges, except where specifically permitted by law.

PART III: DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURES

3.1 Reported violations are investigated by Office of Student Conduct.

3.2 Students are notified in writing of charges and have right to respond.

3.3 Hearings are conducted within 15 business days of charge notice.

3.4 Students have right to advisor but not legal representation at hearings.

3.5 Decisions are based on preponderance of evidence standard.

PART IV: SANCTIONS

4.1 Warning: Written notice that conduct violated the Code.

4.2 Probation: Probationary status with restrictions on activities. Further violations during probation result in more severe sanctions.

4.3 Suspension: Separation from the University for defined period. Tuition is not refunded. Suspended students are banned from campus.

4.4 Expulsion: Permanent separation from the University. Transcript reflects expulsion. Expelled students may never re-enroll.

4.5 Fines: Monetary penalties ranging from $50 to $1,000 depending on violation.

4.6 Restitution: Compensation for damages or losses.

4.7 Educational Sanctions: Required workshops, community service, or counseling.

PART V: APPEALS

5.1 Decisions may be appealed within 5 business days to the Appeals Board.

5.2 Appeals are limited to procedural errors, new evidence, or disproportionate sanctions.

5.3 Appeal decisions are final.

PART VI: SPECIAL PROVISIONS

6.1 Interim suspension may be imposed immediately for serious threats to safety.

6.2 Multiple violations in single incident may result in separate charges.

6.3 Organizations may be held accountable for member conduct at organization events.

6.4 Parents may be notified of alcohol or drug violations for students under 21.

PART VII: RECORDS

Disciplinary records are maintained for seven years. Expulsion records are permanent.
