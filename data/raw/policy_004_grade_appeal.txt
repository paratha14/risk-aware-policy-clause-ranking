GRADE APPEAL POLICY

Document Number: GAP-2024-R2
Last Updated: January 2025

ARTICLE I: GENERAL PROVISIONS

1.1 This policy establishes procedures for students who believe their final course grade was assigned incorrectly or unfairly.

1.2 Students have the right to appeal final grades but must have substantive grounds for the appeal.

1.3 Disagreement with an instructor's judgment alone is not grounds for an appeal.

ARTICLE II: GROUNDS FOR APPEAL

2.1 Acceptable grounds include: mathematical error in grade calculation, application of standards different from those stated in the syllabus, or discrimination prohibited by law.

2.2 The burden of proof rests with the student filing the appeal.

2.3 Appeals based solely on desire for a higher grade will be summarily dismissed.

ARTICLE III: INFORMAL RESOLUTION

3.1 Students must first attempt to resolve the dispute directly with the instructor within 10 business days of grade posting.

3.2 If unresolved, students must contact the department chair within 5 business days.

3.3 Failure to follow this sequence will result in dismissal of any formal appeal.

ARTICLE IV: FORMAL APPEAL PROCESS

4.1 Formal written appeals must be submitted to the Academic Appeals Committee within 30 calendar days of the grade posting.

4.2 Late appeals will not be accepted under any circumstances.

4.3 Appeals must include all relevant documentation and a detailed written statement.

4.4 Incomplete appeals will be returned without review.

ARTICLE V: COMMITTEE REVIEW

5.1 The Committee will render a decision within 45 days of receiving a complete appeal.

5.2 The Committee may uphold the grade, change the grade, or order a re-evaluation.

5.3 Grade increases and decreases are both possible outcomes of appeals.

5.4 Committee decisions are final and binding with no further appeal.

ARTICLE VI: GRADE CHANGES

6.1 Grade changes can only be made through official channels.

6.2 Instructors who attempt to change grades outside the official process face disciplinary action.

6.3 Students who attempt to coerce instructors to change grades face academic misconduct charges.

ARTICLE VII: RECORD RETENTION

7.1 All appeal documents are retained for seven years.

7.2 Patterns of frivolous appeals may result in restrictions on future appeal rights.

ARTICLE VIII: SPECIAL CIRCUMSTANCES

8.1 Graduating seniors must file appeals within 15 days to prevent delay of graduation.

8.2 Failure to meet expedited deadlines results in degree conferral with the current grade.

8.3 International students on F-1 visas must maintain GPA requirements and should appeal immediately if grades jeopardize status.
