COURSE ATTENDANCE AND PARTICIPATION POLICY

Policy ID: CP-ATT-2024
Effective: Fall 2024 Semester

1. ATTENDANCE REQUIREMENTS

1.1 Students are expected to attend all scheduled class sessions.

1.2 Missing more than 20% of class sessions may result in automatic failure of the course.

1.3 Three unexcused absences will result in a warning letter sent to the student.

1.4 Six unexcused absences will result in administrative withdrawal from the course with a grade of WF (Withdrawn Failing).

2. EXCUSED ABSENCES

2.1 Documented medical emergencies require a note from a healthcare provider within 48 hours of return.

2.2 University-sanctioned activities require advance notification and documentation from the sponsoring department.

2.3 Religious observances must be reported to the instructor within the first two weeks of the semester.

2.4 Students failing to provide documentation within required timeframes forfeit the excuse.

3. MAKEUP WORK

3.1 Students with excused absences may make up missed assignments within one week of return.

3.2 No makeup opportunities are provided for unexcused absences.

3.3 Final examinations cannot be rescheduled except in cases of documented emergency.

3.4 Requests for exam rescheduling must be approved by both the instructor and department chair.

4. LATE ARRIVAL AND EARLY DEPARTURE

4.1 Arriving more than 10 minutes after class starts counts as an absence.

4.2 Leaving class early without instructor permission counts as an absence.

4.3 Repeated late arrivals may result in additional penalties at instructor discretion.

5. PARTICIPATION GRADES

5.1 Many courses include participation as 10-20% of the final grade.

5.2 Absences directly impact participation grades and cannot be made up.

5.3 Students who miss presentations are awarded a zero for that assignment.

6. ONLINE AND HYBRID COURSES

6.1 Online attendance is tracked through learning management system engagement.

6.2 Failure to complete weekly online activities counts as an absence.

6.3 Students must log in at least twice weekly to maintain attendance.

7. PENALTIES FOR VIOLATIONS

7.1 Administrative withdrawal results in loss of tuition without refund.

7.2 Habitual attendance problems may result in academic probation.

7.3 Students on probation who violate attendance policies face suspension.
