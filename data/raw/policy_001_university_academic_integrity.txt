UNIVERSITY ACADEMIC INTEGRITY POLICY

Policy Number: AI-2024-001
Effective Date: August 1, 2024
Last Revised: January 15, 2025

1. GENERAL POLICY STATEMENT

All students at this University must adhere to the highest standards of academic integrity. This policy sets forth the expectations for honest academic conduct and establishes procedures for addressing violations.

2. DEFINITIONS

2.1 Academic Dishonesty includes but is not limited to cheating, plagiarism, fabrication, and facilitation of dishonest conduct.

2.2 Plagiarism is the use of another person's words, ideas, or data without proper acknowledgment.

2.3 Cheating includes unauthorized use of materials, information, or study aids during any academic exercise.

3. PROHIBITED CONDUCT

3.1 Students shall not submit work that is not their own original creation.

3.2 Unauthorized collaboration on individual assignments is strictly prohibited.

3.3 The use of artificial intelligence tools without explicit instructor permission constitutes a violation of this policy.

3.4 Students must not access or distribute examination materials without authorization.

4. PENALTIES AND SANCTIONS

4.1 First Offense: A failing grade (zero) for the assignment and required meeting with the instructor. The incident will be reported to the Academic Integrity Office.

4.2 Second Offense: Automatic failure (F) for the entire course. The student will be required to complete an academic integrity workshop.

4.3 Third Offense: Suspension from the University for one semester. The student's academic record will reflect the suspension.

4.4 Severe Violations: Cases involving falsification of official documents, forgery, or theft of examinations may result in immediate expulsion from the University.

5. REPORTING OBLIGATIONS

5.1 All instructors must report suspected violations to the Academic Integrity Office within five business days of discovery.

5.2 Students who witness violations are strongly encouraged to report them through the anonymous reporting system.

5.3 Failure to report known violations may itself constitute a violation of this policy.

6. PROCEDURAL REQUIREMENTS

6.1 Students accused of violations have the right to a hearing before the Academic Integrity Board.

6.2 Students may have an advisor present during hearings, but legal representation is not permitted.

6.3 All proceedings shall be kept confidential except as required by law.

7. APPEALS PROCESS

7.1 Students may appeal decisions within ten business days of notification.

7.2 Appeals must be submitted in writing with supporting documentation to the Dean of Students.

7.3 Appeal decisions are final and cannot be further appealed within the University.

8. SPECIAL PROVISIONS

8.1 International students found in violation may face additional immigration consequences.

8.2 Students receiving financial aid may lose eligibility if expelled for academic integrity violations.

8.3 Graduate students face more stringent penalties due to their advanced standing.

9. COURSE SYLLABUS REQUIREMENTS

9.1 Every course syllabus must include this policy or reference to it.

9.2 Instructors must discuss academic integrity expectations during the first week of classes.

9.3 Assignment instructions must clearly state what collaboration is permitted.

10. EDUCATION AND PREVENTION

10.1 All incoming students must complete an online academic integrity module before registration.

10.2 The University shall provide regular workshops and resources on proper citation and academic honesty.

11. RECORD RETENTION

11.1 All violation records shall be maintained for seven years after graduation or separation from the University.

11.2 Records of expelled students shall be maintained permanently.

12. EFFECTIVE DATE AND AMENDMENTS

This policy is effective immediately upon publication. The University reserves the right to modify this policy at any time with proper notice to the community.
