HEALTHCARE PRIVACY AND HIPAA COMPLIANCE POLICY

Policy Code: HIPAA-2024-001
Version: 5.0
Effective: January 1, 2025

SECTION 1: PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY

1.1 This policy ensures compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) Privacy and Security Rules.

1.2 All workforce members who handle Protected Health Information (PHI) must comply with this policy.

1.3 HIPAA violations may result in civil penalties up to $50,000 per violation and criminal penalties including imprisonment.

SECTION 2: PROTECTED HEALTH INFORMATION DEFINITION

2.1 PHI includes any individually identifiable health information created, received, or maintained by the organization.

2.2 PHI includes but is not limited to: names, addresses, dates, telephone numbers, email addresses, Social Security numbers, medical record numbers, health plan beneficiary numbers, biometric identifiers, photographs, and any other unique identifying information.

SECTION 3: USE AND DISCLOSURE RESTRICTIONS

3.1 PHI may only be used or disclosed for treatment, payment, healthcare operations, or as otherwise permitted by law.

3.2 Minimum necessary standard applies: access only the minimum PHI needed to accomplish the intended purpose.

3.3 Unauthorized access to PHI, even without disclosure, is a violation subject to disciplinary action up to termination.

3.4 Accessing PHI of family members, friends, celebrities, or coworkers without legitimate work purpose is strictly prohibited and will result in termination.

SECTION 4: PATIENT RIGHTS

4.1 Patients have the right to access their medical records. Requests must be fulfilled within 30 days.

4.2 Failure to provide access within 30 days without valid reason may result in OCR enforcement action.

4.3 Patients may request amendments to their records. Requests must be responded to within 60 days.

4.4 Patients must be provided with a Notice of Privacy Practices at first service encounter.

SECTION 5: SECURITY SAFEGUARDS

5.1 Physical Safeguards: PHI in any form must be secured when not in use. Computer screens must be locked when leaving workstation.

5.2 Technical Safeguards: Electronic PHI (ePHI) must be encrypted. Access controls and audit logs are required.

5.3 Administrative Safeguards: Workforce training, policies and procedures, and security management processes must be implemented.

5.4 Leaving PHI visible or unsecured results in written warning for first offense, suspension for second offense, and termination for third offense.

SECTION 6: BREACH NOTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS

6.1 Any suspected breach of PHI must be reported to the Privacy Officer within 1 hour of discovery.

6.2 Breaches affecting 500 or more individuals must be reported to HHS and media within 60 days.

6.3 Breaches affecting fewer than 500 individuals must be reported to HHS annually.

6.4 Failure to report breaches in a timely manner may result in additional penalties and workforce member termination.

SECTION 7: BUSINESS ASSOCIATE AGREEMENTS

7.1 Business Associates who handle PHI on our behalf must sign Business Associate Agreements before accessing PHI.

7.2 Engaging vendors without proper BAAs results in organizational liability and employee disciplinary action.

7.3 Business Associate violations may result in contract termination.

SECTION 8: TRAINING REQUIREMENTS

8.1 All workforce members must complete HIPAA training within first 30 days of employment.

8.2 Annual HIPAA refresher training is mandatory.

8.3 Failure to complete training results in system access suspension until training is completed.

8.4 Training completion must be documented and records retained for 6 years.

SECTION 9: SANCTIONS FOR VIOLATIONS

9.1 Violations are categorized by severity:
   - Tier 1 (Unknowing): Civil penalty $100-$50,000 per violation.
   - Tier 2 (Reasonable cause): $1,000-$50,000 per violation.
   - Tier 3 (Willful neglect, corrected): $10,000-$50,000 per violation.
   - Tier 4 (Willful neglect, not corrected): $50,000 per violation, minimum penalty.

9.2 Criminal penalties: Up to $50,000 and 1 year imprisonment for unknowing violations; up to $100,000 and 5 years for violations under false pretenses; up to $250,000 and 10 years for violations with intent to sell or use PHI for personal gain.

9.3 Internal sanctions for workforce members:
   - First minor violation: Written warning and mandatory retraining.
   - Second violation: 5-day suspension without pay.
   - Third violation or any serious violation: Immediate termination.
   - Criminal violations: Termination and referral to law enforcement.

SECTION 10: PATIENT COMMUNICATION

10.1 Do not discuss PHI in public areas, elevators, or where unauthorized persons may overhear.

10.2 Verify patient identity before discussing PHI by telephone.

10.3 Leave only minimum necessary information on voicemail messages.

10.4 Do not send PHI via unencrypted email.

SECTION 11: MOBILE DEVICES AND REMOTE ACCESS

11.1 PHI on mobile devices must be encrypted.

11.2 Lost or stolen devices containing PHI must be reported immediately.

11.3 Remote access to PHI requires VPN and multi-factor authentication.

11.4 Saving PHI to personal devices is strictly prohibited.

SECTION 12: DISPOSAL OF PHI

12.1 Paper records containing PHI must be shredded, not placed in regular trash.

12.2 Electronic media must be wiped or destroyed before disposal.

12.3 Improper disposal of PHI may result in breach notification obligations and sanctions.

SECTION 13: MARKETING AND FUNDRAISING

13.1 Using PHI for marketing purposes requires patient authorization.

13.2 Patients have right to opt out of fundraising communications.

13.3 Unauthorized use of PHI for marketing results in immediate termination.

SECTION 14: INCIDENT REPORTING

Workforce members must report suspected HIPAA violations or security incidents immediately through the incident reporting system. Retaliation against reporters is prohibited.
