TITLE IX SEXUAL HARASSMENT AND DISCRIMINATION POLICY

Policy Code: TIX-2024
Version: 6.0
Effective Date: August 14, 2024

ARTICLE I: POLICY STATEMENT

1.1 The University prohibits discrimination on the basis of sex in its education programs and activities.

1.2 Sexual harassment, including sexual assault, stalking, and dating/domestic violence, is prohibited.

1.3 This policy applies to all students, faculty, staff, and third parties participating in University programs.

ARTICLE II: SCOPE AND JURISDICTION

2.1 This policy applies to conduct occurring in the University's education program or activity in the United States.

2.2 Education program includes locations, events, or circumstances over which the University exercised substantial control.

2.3 Off-campus conduct may be addressed under this policy if it occurred in University education program.

ARTICLE III: PROHIBITED CONDUCT

3.1 Sexual Harassment: Conduct on the basis of sex including: (1) quid pro quo harassment by employee; (2) unwelcome conduct that reasonable person would find so severe or pervasive and objectively offensive that it denies equal access to education program; or (3) sexual assault, dating violence, domestic violence, or stalking.

3.2 Sexual Assault: Non-consensual sexual contact or non-consensual sexual intercourse.

3.3 Stalking: Engaging in course of conduct directed at specific person that would cause reasonable person to fear for safety or suffer substantial emotional distress.

3.4 Dating Violence: Violence committed by person in romantic or intimate relationship with victim.

3.5 Domestic Violence: Violence committed by current or former spouse, intimate partner, or person with whom victim shares child.

ARTICLE IV: REPORTING

4.1 Reports may be made to Title IX Coordinator, Deputy Coordinators, or online reporting system 24/7.

4.2 Employees with authority to institute corrective measures (administrators, faculty, advisors) are required to report known incidents to Title IX Coordinator.

4.3 Failure of responsible employee to report may result in disciplinary action up to termination.

4.4 Confidential resources (counselors, health services, victim advocates) are not required to report.

ARTICLE V: SUPPORTIVE MEASURES

5.1 Complainants and respondents offered supportive measures to restore or preserve access to education program.

5.2 Measures may include no-contact orders, schedule changes, academic modifications, housing modifications, or interim suspension in extreme cases.

5.3 Supportive measures are non-disciplinary and non-punitive.

5.4 Violation of no-contact order is independent violation and may result in suspension or expulsion.

ARTICLE VI: EMERGENCY REMOVAL

6.1 Respondent may be removed immediately if individualized safety and risk analysis determines immediate threat to physical health or safety.

6.2 Respondent must be provided notice and opportunity to challenge removal within 5 days.

6.3 Emergency removal decisions made by Student Conduct Officer in consultation with Title IX Coordinator.

ARTICLE VII: FORMAL COMPLAINT PROCESS

7.1 Formal complaint initiates investigation and adjudication process.

7.2 Complainant must be participating or attempting to participate in University education program at time of filing.

7.3 Title IX Coordinator may initiate formal complaint in limited circumstances.

7.4 Parties have equal rights throughout process including advisors, access to evidence, and appeal.

ARTICLE VIII: INVESTIGATION

8.1 Trained investigator assigned to gather evidence.

8.2 Both parties have equal opportunity to present witnesses and evidence.

8.3 Medical and counseling records cannot be accessed without voluntary consent.

8.4 Parties receive draft investigation report for review and response.

8.5 Final investigation report summarizes relevant evidence.

8.6 Investigation completed within 90 days absent extenuating circumstances.

ARTICLE IX: HEARING PROCESS

9.1 Live hearing conducted with decision-maker separate from Title IX Coordinator and investigator.

9.2 Parties have right to advisor who may conduct cross-examination.

9.3 University provides advisor if party does not have one.

9.4 Cross-examination conducted by advisors, not parties personally.

9.5 Decision-maker cannot rely on statements not subject to cross-examination.

9.6 Relevancy determinations made in real-time during hearing.

ARTICLE X: STANDARD OF PROOF

10.1 Preponderance of evidence standard applies (more likely than not).

10.2 Same standard applied to formal complaints against students and employees.

ARTICLE XI: SANCTIONS

11.1 For Students:
  - Warning: Written notice of violation.
  - Probation: Restrictions on participation for defined period. Further violations during probation result in suspension.
  - Suspension: Separation from University for defined period (typically 1-4 years). Suspended students banned from campus.
  - Expulsion: Permanent separation. Transcript reflects expulsion.
  
11.2 For Employees:
  - Warning: Written reprimand placed in file.
  - Suspension: Unpaid leave for defined period.
  - Termination: Employment ended immediately.
  - Tenure does not protect from termination for serious violations.

11.3 Aggravating factors (use of force, prior violations, retaliation) increase sanctions.

11.4 Pattern of sexual misconduct typically results in expulsion for students or termination for employees.

ARTICLE XII: APPEALS

12.1 Either party may appeal on following grounds: procedural irregularity, new evidence not reasonably available at time of determination, or Title IX personnel had conflict of interest or bias.

12.2 Appeals must be filed within 5 business days of decision notice.

12.3 Opposite party given opportunity to respond to appeal.

12.4 Appeal decided by different decision-maker than original.

12.5 Appeal decision is final.

ARTICLE XIII: RETALIATION PROHIBITED

13.1 Retaliation against any person for reporting, participating in investigation, or exercising rights under this policy is prohibited.

13.2 Retaliation includes intimidation, threats, coercion, discrimination, or adverse action.

13.3 Retaliation is independent violation and may result in sanctions even if underlying complaint not substantiated.

13.4 Retaliation violations may result in expulsion or termination.

ARTICLE XIV: FALSE STATEMENTS

14.1 Knowingly making false statements or submitting false information is prohibited.

14.2 Good faith reports that are ultimately unsubstantiated are not false statements.

14.3 False statements may result in disciplinary action.

ARTICLE XV: CONFIDENTIALITY

15.1 University will keep identities of parties and witnesses confidential except as necessary for investigation or required by law.

15.2 Parties should maintain confidentiality to protect integrity of process.

15.3 Parties permitted to discuss case with advisors, family, and confidential resources.

ARTICLE XVI: EDUCATION AND PREVENTION

All incoming students and new employees must complete Title IX training within first 30 days. Campus climate surveys conducted biennially.
