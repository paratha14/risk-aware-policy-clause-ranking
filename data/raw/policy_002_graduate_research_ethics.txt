GRADUATE STUDENT RESEARCH ETHICS AND CONDUCT POLICY

Document ID: GR-ETH-2024
Version: 3.0
Date Effective: September 1, 2024

SECTION 1: SCOPE AND PURPOSE

This policy governs all research activities conducted by graduate students enrolled in thesis, dissertation, or research-intensive programs. All graduate students must comply with these standards.

SECTION 2: RESEARCH INTEGRITY PRINCIPLES

2.1 Honesty: Students shall report data truthfully and accurately without fabrication or falsification.

2.2 Objectivity: Research must be conducted without bias or manipulation of results to support predetermined conclusions.

2.3 Responsible Conduct: Students must follow all applicable regulations, protocols, and ethical guidelines.

SECTION 3: DATA MANAGEMENT AND RECORD KEEPING

3.1 All raw data must be retained for a minimum of ten years after publication or graduation, whichever is later.

3.2 Research notebooks must be maintained according to departmental standards and remain property of the University.

3.3 Students shall not delete, alter, or destroy data after analysis has begun unless documented protocols permit such action.

3.4 Failure to maintain adequate records may result in revocation of degree conferral.

SECTION 4: AUTHORSHIP AND PUBLICATION

4.1 Authorship disputes must be resolved before manuscript submission.

4.2 Students may not submit the same work to multiple publications simultaneously without disclosure.

4.3 Plagiarism in any form, including self-plagiarism without proper citation, is strictly prohibited.

4.4 Gift authorship or honorary authorship is prohibited. All authors must have made substantial contributions.

SECTION 5: HUMAN SUBJECTS RESEARCH

5.1 Research involving human subjects requires IRB approval before commencement. Conducting research without approval will result in immediate termination from the program.

5.2 Informed consent must be obtained from all participants using approved consent forms.

5.3 Breach of participant confidentiality may result in criminal charges in addition to academic penalties.

5.4 Students must complete CITI training before conducting any human subjects research.

SECTION 6: ANIMAL RESEARCH REQUIREMENTS

6.1 All animal research requires IACUC approval. Unapproved animal research is a serious violation subject to legal prosecution.

6.2 Students must follow all approved protocols without deviation.

6.3 Any adverse events must be reported to IACUC within 24 hours.

6.4 Failure to provide appropriate animal care may result in expulsion and criminal charges.

SECTION 7: BIOSAFETY AND HAZARDOUS MATERIALS

7.1 Students working with biohazards must complete required safety training annually.

7.2 Improper disposal of hazardous waste is a violation that may result in suspension.

7.3 All accidents involving hazardous materials must be reported immediately to Environmental Health and Safety.

SECTION 8: CONFLICTS OF INTEREST

8.1 Students must disclose any financial interests related to their research.

8.2 Failure to disclose conflicts may result in retraction of publications and degree revocation.

8.3 Romantic or familial relationships with research participants must be disclosed and may require reassignment of the research project.

SECTION 9: RESEARCH MISCONDUCT DEFINITIONS

9.1 Fabrication: Making up data or results and recording them as genuine.

9.2 Falsification: Manipulating research materials, equipment, or processes, or changing or omitting data.

9.3 Plagiarism: Appropriation of another person's ideas, processes, results, or words without giving appropriate credit.

SECTION 10: PENALTIES FOR MISCONDUCT

10.1 Minor Violations: Written warning and mandatory ethics training.

10.2 Moderate Violations: Suspension from research for up to six months and notation in academic file.

10.3 Serious Violations: Expulsion from graduate program without degree conferral. Student must repay all funding received.

10.4 Severe Violations: Expulsion, degree revocation if already granted, and report to Office of Research Integrity. The student may be barred from future federal research funding.

SECTION 11: INVESTIGATION PROCEDURES

11.1 Allegations must be submitted in writing to the Research Integrity Officer.

11.2 Preliminary inquiry shall be completed within 30 days.

11.3 If warranted, formal investigation shall begin within 10 days of inquiry completion.

11.4 Investigations shall be completed within 120 days unless circumstances require extension.

SECTION 12: WHISTLEBLOWER PROTECTION

12.1 Retaliation against individuals who report misconduct in good faith is strictly prohibited.

12.2 Anonymous reports may be submitted through the University's ethics hotline.

12.3 The identity of reporters shall be kept confidential to the extent permitted by law.

SECTION 13: ADVISOR RESPONSIBILITIES

13.1 Faculty advisors must provide adequate oversight of student research activities.

13.2 Advisors who fail to supervise adequately may share responsibility for student misconduct.

13.3 Advisors must review and approve all manuscripts before submission.

SECTION 14: COLLABORATION GUIDELINES

14.1 Collaborative research agreements must be documented in writing before work begins.

14.2 Students must not share unpublished data without permission from all collaborators.

14.3 Intellectual property rights must be established before research commences.

SECTION 15: ANNUAL CERTIFICATION

All graduate students engaged in research must complete an annual certification acknowledging they have read and understand this policy. Failure to complete certification by the deadline will result in suspension of research privileges.
