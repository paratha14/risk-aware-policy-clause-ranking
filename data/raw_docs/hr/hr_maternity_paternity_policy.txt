MATERNITY AND PATERNITY LEAVE POLICY

1. Maternity Leave Entitlement
All employees are entitled to 52 weeks of maternity leave regardless of length
of service. Statutory Maternity Pay is payable for 39 weeks subject to eligibility
criteria. Denial of maternity leave rights constitutes unlawful discrimination.

2. Notification Requirements
Employees must notify HR of pregnancy and intended maternity leave dates at least
15 weeks before the expected week of childbirth. Failure to notify does not remove
entitlement but may affect pay arrangements.

3. Keeping in Touch Days
Employees may work up to 10 Keeping in Touch days during maternity leave without
losing their entitlement. Compulsion to work during maternity leave beyond KIT
days constitutes a breach of statutory rights.

4. Return to Work
Employees returning from maternity leave have the right to return to the same
job. Where this is not reasonably practicable, a suitable alternative must be
offered. Failure to reinstate on return constitutes automatic unfair dismissal.

5. Paternity Leave
Eligible employees are entitled to 2 weeks of paid paternity leave to be taken
within 56 days of the child's birth. Requests for paternity leave must not be
refused where the employee meets eligibility criteria.
