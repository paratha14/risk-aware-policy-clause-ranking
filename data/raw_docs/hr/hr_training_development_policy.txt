TRAINING AND DEVELOPMENT POLICY

1. Mandatory Training
Employees must complete all mandatory training within the prescribed timescales.
Failure to complete mandatory compliance training (including data protection,
health and safety, and anti-bribery) without a valid reason is a disciplinary
offence.

2. Sponsored Study
Employees receiving financial support for external qualifications must remain
with the company for a minimum period following completion. Resignation before
the required period results in a proportional repayment of training costs.

3. Training Agreements
Employees receiving substantial training investment may be required to sign a
training agreement. Breach of a signed training agreement entitles the company
to recover training costs through deduction from salary or legal proceedings.

4. Time Off for Study
Eligible employees have the statutory right to request reasonable time off for
study or training. This right applies to employees with 26 weeks continuous
service. Unreasonable refusal may give rise to a tribunal complaint.

5. Qualification Misrepresentation
Employees who misrepresent their qualifications or training records will be
subject to disciplinary action including dismissal. Misrepresentation discovered
after employment commences may result in termination for fraud.
