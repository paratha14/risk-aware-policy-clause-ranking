WHISTLEBLOWING POLICY

1. Purpose
This policy enables employees to raise concerns about illegal activity, danger
to health and safety, environmental damage, miscarriages of justice, or deliberate
concealment of any of the above without fear of retaliation.

2. Protection
Employees who make protected disclosures in good faith are protected from
dismissal, demotion, or any other detriment. Retaliation against a whistleblower
constitutes gross misconduct and may give rise to legal liability.

3. Reporting Channels
Concerns may be raised with the line manager, HR Director, or the designated
confidential whistleblowing hotline. Reports may be made anonymously, though
this may limit the ability to investigate fully.

4. Malicious Disclosures
Employees who make disclosures known to be false or made in bad faith are not
protected and may be subject to disciplinary action.

5. Confidentiality
The identity of the whistleblower will be kept confidential to the extent possible.
Breach of whistleblower confidentiality by any staff member is a serious
disciplinary offence.
