CONFLICT RESOLUTION AND MEDIATION POLICY

1. Early Resolution
Line managers are expected to address interpersonal conflicts at the earliest
opportunity. Failure to address known conflicts that escalate into formal
grievances or tribunal claims may be considered a management performance issue.

2. Mediation
The company may offer mediation as an alternative to formal grievance procedures.
Participation in mediation is voluntary. Refusal to engage in offered mediation
is not penalised but will be noted if the matter proceeds to formal proceedings.

3. Formal Grievance
Where early resolution fails, employees may raise a formal grievance. The formal
grievance procedure must be followed before an employment tribunal claim can be
brought in most circumstances.

4. Vexatious Conduct
Employees who repeatedly raise complaints found to be without merit or who
pursue disputes for improper purposes may be subject to disciplinary action
for vexatious behaviour.

5. Non-Retaliation
No employee will suffer detriment for raising a genuine workplace conflict or
participating in conflict resolution procedures. Retaliation constitutes gross
misconduct subject to disciplinary action.
