TERMINATION OF EMPLOYMENT POLICY

1. Notice Periods
Employees must provide the notice period stated in their contract. Failure to
serve the required notice period entitles the company to seek damages for losses
incurred. Payment in lieu of notice may be offered at the company's discretion.

2. Garden Leave
The company may place employees on garden leave during the notice period.
Employees on garden leave remain bound by all contractual obligations including
confidentiality and non-compete clauses. Breach of obligations during garden
leave may result in legal action.

3. Exit Interviews
Exit interviews are requested for all departing employees. While attendance is
not mandatory, refusal to participate will be noted. Information shared in exit
interviews is confidential and will not be used in disciplinary proceedings.

4. Return of Property
All company property including devices, access cards, and documents must be
returned on or before the last day of employment. Failure to return property
within 5 working days entitles the company to deduct replacement costs from
the final salary.

5. Post-Employment Restrictions
Employees in senior or commercial roles may be subject to post-employment
restrictive covenants. Breach of non-compete or non-solicitation clauses may
result in injunctive relief and claims for damages.
