STUDENT VISA ATTENDANCE AND ENGAGEMENT POLICY

1. Mandatory Engagement
Students studying on a student visa must maintain engagement as required by
immigration regulations. The university is required to report non-engaging
students to the Home Office. Failure to engage may result in visa curtailment.

2. Attendance Threshold
A minimum attendance of 80% is required for all visa-holding students. Students
falling below this threshold will receive a formal warning. Continued non-attendance
results in mandatory reporting to immigration authorities.

3. Unauthorized Absence
Absence of more than 10 consecutive days without notification to the international
student office constitutes unauthorized absence and triggers mandatory immigration
reporting.

4. Deferral and Interruption
Students wishing to defer or interrupt their studies must notify the international
student office immediately. Failure to update immigration records during a period
of interruption may have serious consequences for current and future visa applications.

5. End of Studies
Students must leave the country by the visa expiry date following completion of
studies. The university is required by law to report students who remain in the
country beyond their authorized study period.
