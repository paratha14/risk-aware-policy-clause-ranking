EXAMINATION AND ASSESSMENT POLICY

1. Academic Integrity
Students must not engage in any form of academic dishonesty including plagiarism,
fabrication, cheating, contract cheating, or unauthorized collaboration. Violations
are treated as serious misconduct and may result in course failure, suspension,
or permanent expulsion from the university. All violations are recorded on the
student's academic file.

2. Examination Conduct
Mobile phones and unauthorized electronic devices are strictly prohibited during
examinations. Students found in possession of unauthorized materials shall receive
a grade of zero for the entire examination and be referred to the Academic
Integrity Committee for formal investigation.

3. Plagiarism
Presenting another person's work as one's own, whether intentional or unintentional,
constitutes plagiarism. Confirmed plagiarism results in a failing grade for the
assessment. Repeat offences result in academic suspension for a minimum of one
semester.

4. Use of AI Tools
Submission of AI-generated text as original work without disclosure is considered
academic dishonesty. First offences result in assignment failure. Repeat offences
result in course failure and referral to the Disciplinary Committee.

5. Late Submission
Assignments submitted after the deadline without approved extension receive a
deduction of 10% per calendar day. No submission accepted after 7 days without
documented medical or compassionate grounds.

6. Attendance
Students must attend at least 75% of scheduled classes. Students below minimum
attendance are barred from final examinations without appeal.
