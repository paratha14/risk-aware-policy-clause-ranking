THESIS AND DISSERTATION EXAMINATION POLICY

1. Examiner Appointment
External examiners must be independent of the candidate and their supervisors.
Appointment of examiners with a conflict of interest constitutes a procedural
irregularity that may invalidate the examination outcome.

2. Submission Standards
Theses must conform to the prescribed format and word limits. Submissions that
fail to meet formatting requirements may be returned unchecked. Repeated failure
to comply with submission standards may result in the thesis being deemed not
ready for examination.

3. Viva Voce Examination
The viva voce is a compulsory component of postgraduate research degrees.
Failure to attend a scheduled viva without prior written notice and valid cause
results in automatic failure. A second viva attempt is at the discretion of
the Examination Board.

4. Examination Outcomes
Possible outcomes are: pass without corrections, minor corrections (within 3
months), major corrections (within 12 months), resubmission (within 18 months),
or fail with no resubmission. A fail outcome cannot be appealed on grounds of
academic judgment.

5. Plagiarism in Research Degrees
Plagiarism detected in a submitted thesis after award results in revocation of
the degree. The university reserves the right to revoke awards up to 6 years
after conferral upon discovery of academic misconduct.
