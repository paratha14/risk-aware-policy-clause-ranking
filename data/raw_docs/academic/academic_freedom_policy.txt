ACADEMIC FREEDOM AND FREEDOM OF SPEECH POLICY

1. Scope of Academic Freedom
Academic staff and students have the right to question and test received wisdom,
put forward new ideas, and express controversial or unpopular opinions within the
law. This right is protected provided it does not constitute harassment, incitement,
or unlawful discrimination.

2. External Speakers
Invitations to external speakers must be submitted to the university for approval
at least 14 days before the event. Events featuring speakers who may express views
that risk public order or constitute hate speech require enhanced risk assessment.
Unauthorized events may be cancelled without notice.

3. Protest and Demonstration
Peaceful protest on university premises is permitted within designated areas and
requires advance notification to security. Protests that disrupt teaching, research,
or administrative activities will be dispersed and participants may face disciplinary
action.

4. Social Media and Public Comment
Staff and students may comment publicly on matters of academic and public interest.
However, public statements that misrepresent the university's position, disclose
confidential information, or constitute harassment are not protected by academic
freedom principles.

5. Retaliation
Retaliation against any member of the university community for exercising legitimate
academic freedom or freedom of expression is prohibited and constitutes misconduct.
