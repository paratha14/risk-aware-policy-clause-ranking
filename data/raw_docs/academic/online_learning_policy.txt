ONLINE AND DISTANCE LEARNING POLICY

1. Identity Verification
Students participating in online assessments must verify their identity as required.
Allowing another person to complete an online assessment on one's behalf constitutes
contract cheating and will result in expulsion.

2. Assessment Integrity
Online assessments must be completed independently unless group work is explicitly
permitted. Use of unauthorized resources, websites, or AI tools during timed online
assessments is treated as examination misconduct.

3. Technical Issues
Students experiencing technical issues during timed assessments must notify the
module leader within 30 minutes of the issue occurring. Technical issues reported
after assessment submission will not be considered as grounds for appeal.

4. Recording of Sessions
Online teaching sessions may be recorded for students who are unable to attend.
Students must not share recordings outside the enrolled cohort. Redistribution
of teaching recordings constitutes a breach of intellectual property rights.

5. Participation Requirements
Students must participate actively in online discussions and group activities as
required by their programme. Consistent failure to participate may be treated as
non-engagement and reported accordingly.
